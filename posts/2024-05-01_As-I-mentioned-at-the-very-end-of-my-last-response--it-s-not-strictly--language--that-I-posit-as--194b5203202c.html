Published on May 01, 2024 at 03:17 PM

As I mentioned at the very end of my last response, it’s not strictly ‘language’ that I posit as necessary for subjective thought. It’s ‘any’ communication (i.e. body language, touch—even pain itself).

These matters would therefore be on a spectrum.

For example, someone that is deaf and dumb can still communicate though touch.

However, a hypothetical living ‘brain in a jar’, who never learned anything at all, and was born as a brain in a jar, and unable to communicate in any way, I would argue, has no consciousness, nor does it possess subjective experiences. It is simply a brain in a jar—without the ability to acquire subjective thoughts or consciousness.

If you’re so inclined to read Jaynes’s work, I predict that he will very soon become much more widely cited. His work seems to align with my own recent findings, some of which I have already shared.
