Breaking the Chains: Overcoming Trauma Bonding to Escape a Narcissist

Reclaiming Your Life and Future from Narcissistic Abuse

By recognizing common signs of trauma bonding and employing strategies like education, no contact and self-care, you can overcome conditioning and break free of a narcissistic relationship.

When you’re trauma bonded to a narcissistic partner, leaving the relationship can feel agonizingly difficult. But by recognizing the signs and employing key strategies, you can break free.

What is trauma bonding? It occurs when a victim forms strong emotional ties with their abuser due to the cycle of abuse. The intermittent pain and positive reinforcement creates powerful psychological conditioning.

In my marriage to a narcissist, I became trauma bonded without realizing it. Her periodic discarding and hoovering kept me addicted to the highs.

While I didn’t always believe her false narratives, I justified her behavior because I believed she suffered from a treatable, undiagnosed mental illness.

Her narcissistic traits, while apparent later, never entered my mind while I was enmeshed in a relationship with her. I simply craved the initial experience of idealized love.

I didn’t want to believe that those initial feelings — our love story — was part of a spectacular, impossibly prolific whopper of a lie.

So, for years, I lived in denial. I hoped she would heal, get treatment, take medication — anything to return to the love I once believed she felt for me.

This was part of my trauma bonding experience that kept me clinging on to our marriage. I simply didn’t want to believe the person I love, married, and had a child with, never really exited.

Other’s may experience this differently.

Here are signs of trauma bonding that you may recognize:

  • Feeling responsible for their happiness and behavior
  • Excusing their actions or blaming yourself
  • Doubting your own instincts and perception
  • Isolating from friends/family to please them
  • Obsessively seeking their validation or love

You can break the trauma bond by:

  • Education — Learn about narcissistic manipulation techniques like gaslighting so you can anticipate their moves and not fall prey. Knowledge is power.
  • No Contact — Cut ties completely so their hoovering cannot reel you back in. Limit contact to only essentials like legal proceedings.
  • Support Network — Surround yourself with people who can validate your experiences and provide strength. You don’t have to do this alone.
  • Self-Care — Make your physical/emotional health a priority to manage the trauma and stress. Therapy can help process unresolved emotions.
  • Set Boundaries — Maintain firm boundaries if you cannot go no contact. Expect manipulations but refuse to engage.

With determination and the right strategies, you can reclaim your life, heal, and build the healthy relationships you deserve.

My chains shattered the moment I committed to choose me, instead of my ex partner’s false self.

Your chains can be broken too.
