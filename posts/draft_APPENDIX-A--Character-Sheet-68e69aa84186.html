APPENDIX A— Character Sheet

Published on


In a realm where magic and deceit intertwine, Seraphina, a tiefling prodigy raised by con artists, faces a pivotal choice between the moral high ground and her own burgeoning ambition. With a natural talent for manipulation and a growing sense of self-importance, she treads a dark path, torn between maintaining her ethical compass and embracing her destiny as a feared villainess.





APPENDIX A— Character Sheet

Young Seraphina

“In every shadow, a secret; in every giggle, a trap.”

Race: Tiefling
Class: Young Rogue (Budding Arcane Trickster)
Alignment: Chaotic Neutral (leaning towards Chaotic Evil)
Background: Charlatan Prodigy

Appearance:

  • Youthful yet piercing gaze, eyes dancing with mischief.
  • Petite, with nimble fingers perfect for pilfering small items.
  • A mischievous grin that’s both endearing and unsettling.
  • Hair a cascading wave of deep blue, often braided with shiny trinkets of dubious origin.

Abilities and Skills:

  • Intelligence: Sharp and curious, with a knack for solving puzzles and creating intricate plans.
  • Dexterity: Agile and adept at acrobatics, showing early signs of a master thief.
  • Charisma: Naturally charming, drawing others in with a mix of innocence and guile.

Feats and Talents:

  • Prankster Par Excellence: Skilled in playing tricks and pranks, leaving her peers both annoyed and amazed.
  • Budding Illusionist: Shows a natural affinity for creating minor but convincing illusions.
  • Quick-Witted: Remarkably fast in coming up with plausible lies and creative solutions.
  • Escape Artist in Training: Exhibits an uncanny ability to wriggle out of tight situations, both physically and socially.

Magical Abilities (Latent):

  • Illusionary Flare: Beginning to manipulate light and shadow to create simple illusions.
  • Minor Mind Tricks: Shows early signs of being able to influence thoughts and emotions subtly.
  • Telekinetic Tendencies: Occasionally moves small objects with her mind, mostly unconsciously.

Equipment:

  • A small, jewel-encrusted dagger, more ornamental than functional.
  • A pouch of colorful powders and smoke pellets for her tricks and illusions.
  • A collection of stolen trinkets, each with its own naughty story.

Personality Traits:

  • Confident to the point of arrogance in her abilities and intellect.
  • Relishes the thrill of the con, the game of deceit.
  • Often underestimates the danger, believing she can talk or trick her way out of anything.
  • Finds humor in situations, sometimes ignoring the seriousness of the consequences.

Flaws:

  • Overconfidence in her schemes and tricks.
  • Struggles with empathy, viewing others as pawns in her games.
  • Impulsive and easily bored, seeking constant stimulation and challenge.

Goals (Childhood):

  • To become the greatest trickster and illusionist ever known.
  • To accumulate a trove of secrets and treasures through her cunning.
  • To prove herself against the adults and authority figures who underestimate her.

Allies and Enemies:

  • A small gang of other mischievous children, drawn to her charisma and fearlessness.
  • Teachers and adults, both frustrated and impressed by her cleverness and audacity.
  • A rival child prodigy, equally cunning and a constant challenger to her schemes.

Backstory:

Born into a family of con artists and illusionists, Seraphina was immersed in the art of deception from her earliest days. Her parents, proud of their heritage, encouraged her precocious talents, unknowingly nurturing her nascent narcissism. The thrill of successful cons, the applause for her illusions, and the joy of outsmarting adults reinforced her belief in her own superiority. She quickly grew to see life as a grand game, one she was destined to win. Her early experiences with betrayal, both as perpetrator and victim, began to warp her understanding of trust and loyalty, setting the stage for her chaotic and manipulative future.

APPENDIX B — Flash Fiction

Title: The Great Cookie Caper

In the quaint village of Mirrormere, where every path seemed to whisper secrets and every shadow held a tale, there lived a three-year-old tiefling girl named Seraphina. With hair as blue as the midnight sky and eyes shimmering with unspoken mischief, she was as enchanting as she was troublesome.

One sunny afternoon, as the village bazaar buzzed with activity, Seraphina toddled through the crowd, her tiny hands clasping a ragged doll. Her mother, preoccupied with bartering for spices, didn’t notice the gleam in her daughter’s eyes as she gazed at the bakery stand.

There, atop a glistening display, lay the most magnificent cookie Seraphina had ever seen. It was as large as her little face, studded with chocolate chips and nuts, a prize worth any risk.

With the stealth only a child could muster, Seraphina approached the stand. The baker, a burly man with a jolly laugh, was busy attending to a group of chattering women. No one paid any mind to the tiny figure slipping under the table.

Heart pounding with excitement, Seraphina emerged behind the stand. Standing on her tiptoes, she reached for the cookie. Her fingers brushed against the tantalizing treat when suddenly — 

“Hey! What do you think you’re doing?” bellowed the baker, spotting the tiny intruder. Seraphina froze, her hand still outstretched. The bazaar fell silent, all eyes turning to the spectacle.

Panicked, Seraphina did the only thing she could think of. She let out a wail, tears springing to her eyes as if by magic. The crowd’s mood shifted instantly. Sympathetic murmurs filled the air.

“Oh, the poor dear must be lost!” exclaimed one of the women.

“Look how she’s crying! She’s scared,” added another.

The baker, his stern expression softening, bent down to Seraphina’s level. “Hey, little one, no need to cry. Where’s your mother?”

Through her tears, Seraphina pointed vaguely towards the crowd. The baker, now completely disarmed, handed her the very cookie she had been trying to steal.

“Here, take this. No tears now,” he said gently.

As the baker escorted her back to her mother, Seraphina’s tears magically ceased. Clutching the stolen prize, she couldn’t help but smile. Even at the tender age of three, Seraphina had learned the power of deception, and the thrill of getting away with it. Little did the villagers know, this was just the beginning of the many capers of Seraphina, the future Archduchess Trickster of Mirrormere.

APPENDIX C — Flash Fiction

Title: The Vanishing Vase

At the age of five, Seraphina, the tiefling child of mischief, had already earned a reputation in her village, even after only a few short weeks. Her eyes sparkled with untamed curiosity, and her laughter often echoed through the cobbled streets, a harbinger of playful chaos.

On a breezy afternoon in the heart of spring, the village celebrated the Festival of Blossoms. Colorful flowers adorned every home, and the air was filled with sweet fragrances and joyful music. It was during this festival that Lady Elowen, a noblewoman known for her exquisite collection of artifacts, displayed her prized possession: an ancient, delicate vase said to be as old as Mirrormere itself.

Seraphina, with her keen eyes and adventurous spirit, was immediately drawn to the vase. It shimmered in hues of blue and green, with intricate patterns that seemed to dance in the sunlight. The vase was placed on a pedestal in the center of the town square, guarded by two stern-looking sentries.

“Remember, children, look with your eyes, not with your hands,” Lady Elowen announced to the gathering crowd. Seraphina, standing in the front, nodded along with the other children, her mind already weaving a tapestry of plots.

As the festival proceeded, Seraphina waited patiently for her moment. When a troupe of dancers began their performance, drawing the crowd’s attention, she knew her time had come. With the crowd distracted and the guards swaying to the music, Seraphina slipped away unnoticed.

She approached the vase, her heart racing with excitement. Just as she was about to touch it, a voice boomed, “Stop right there!” It was one of the guards, his eyes fixed on her.

In a split second, Seraphina did something unexpected. She started dancing, her tiny feet moving to the rhythm of the music, a beaming smile on her face. The guard, taken aback, let out a reluctant chuckle.

“You should be with the other children, dancing,” he said, his stern expression softening.

With a giggle, Seraphina twirled away, joining a group of dancing children. The guard, now thoroughly charmed, turned his attention back to the vase.

But Seraphina wasn’t done yet. As she danced, she skillfully edged closer to the vase. Then, with a swift, fluid motion only a child could manage, she snatched a fallen blossom from the ground and gently tossed it onto the vase.

The crowd gasped as the blossom landed perfectly on the rim, adding to the vase’s beauty. Lady Elowen, surprised and delighted, clapped her hands.

“What a marvelous addition! A child’s touch, innocent and pure,” she exclaimed, her eyes sparkling with joy.

The crowd erupted in applause, and Seraphina, now the star of the festival, took a graceful bow. As she rejoined her mother in the crowd, no one noticed the triumphant glint in her eyes or the slight smudge of dirt on her fingers from the blossom.

That day, Seraphina learned the power of a well-timed distraction and the art of blending innocence with mischief. And thus, another chapter was added to the early legends of Seraphina, the future Archduchess Trickster, whose mind was as bright as her heart was wild.

APPENDIX D— Flash Fiction

In the wandering caravans of the traveling folk, among the transient communities that roamed the outskirts of a major city, there lived a young tiefling girl named Seraphina. Known for her mischievous smile and eyes gleaming with a cunning beyond her years, Seraphina and her family were as elusive as the winds, never staying long enough in one place to be fully understood.

During a vibrant spring, the caravans halted near the village of Elderglen for the Festival of Blossoms. Elderglen, a picturesque settlement nestled in a valley, was famed for its annual celebration, where flowers in a myriad of colors bloomed in every nook and cranny.

At the heart of the festival was a competition to create the most beautiful flower arrangement. The centerpiece was a spectacular, ancient vase, provided by a local benefactor. It stood proudly in the village square, drawing admirers from far and wide.

Seraphina, with her innate curiosity, was captivated by the vase. Its surface danced with colors and patterns that seemed to tell a story of the ages. Guarded by villagers who took turns watching over it, the vase was an alluring challenge to the young trickster.

As the festival buzzed with energy, Seraphina’s parents were busy charming the locals with their exotic wares and captivating stories. Using this to her advantage, Seraphina weaved through the crowd, her small frame easily slipping unnoticed.

She approached the vase, her heart skipping with excitement. Just as she reached out, a stern voice stopped her. “This is not a toy, little one,” said the village elder, a kind but firm woman who had caught Seraphina’s delicate hand in the act.

With an impish grin, Seraphina responded, “I just wanted to make it prettier!” Her innocence was disarming, and the elder’s expression softened.

“Well, then,” said the elder, intrigued. “Show us.”

Seraphina, seizing the moment, picked a wildflower from the ground — a simple daisy. With a dramatic flair, she placed it delicately atop the vase. The simplicity of the act, the purity of a child’s touch amongst the elaborate arrangements, caught everyone’s attention.

The crowd, initially taken aback, erupted in applause. The elder, now smiling widely, proclaimed, “This child has reminded us that sometimes, beauty lies in simplicity.”

Seraphina, basking in her moment of glory, returned to her parents, who watched with a mix of pride and amusement. They knew, as did she, that this was more than a playful gesture. It was a testament to her cleverness and charm, traits that would shape her destiny.

As the caravans departed Elderglen the next day, stories of the tiefling child who enchanted the Festival of Blossoms spread through the village. Little did they know, they had witnessed the early cunning of Seraphina, the future Archduchess Trickster, whose journey through Mirrormere was just beginning.

APPENDIX E — Flash Fiction

Title: The Whispering Willow Caper

In the rolling meadows of Mirrormere, where the hills whispered ancient secrets and the rivers sang melodies of old, the caravans of the transient folk found solace. Among them was Seraphina, now seven, her reputation as a cunning trickster blossoming like the wildflowers around her.

The caravans had settled near the small, sleepy hamlet of Willowshade, known for its towering willow trees and a legendary statue called the Whispering Willow. This statue, carved from an ancient tree, was said to hold the wisdom of the ages, and its words were whispered only once a year during the Moonlit Festival.

As the festival approached, Seraphina’s eyes sparkled with the thrill of a new challenge. The Whispering Willow was guarded by an ancient puzzle, a series of locks and mechanisms that had stumped many. It was said that only a mind both innocent and cunning could unravel its secrets.

On the night of the festival, as the moon cast its silvery glow over Willowshade, the villagers gathered around the Whispering Willow, their attempts to unlock its secrets failing one by one. Seraphina, cloaked in the shadows, observed each attempt with keen interest.

“Why don’t you try, Seraphina?” her mother nudged, knowing well her daughter’s penchant for puzzles.

Stepping forward, Seraphina approached the statue. The villagers watched, a mix of curiosity and skepticism in their eyes. The statue, bathed in moonlight, stood silent and imposing.

Seraphina’s fingers danced over the locks, her mind weaving through the puzzle like a spider through its web. The locks clicked, the gears turned, and the statue began to whisper. The crowd gasped in awe as Seraphina, with a triumphant smile, stepped back.

The statue’s whisper was a riddle, a poetic verse that spoke of the balance between man and nature. Seraphina listened, her eyes reflecting the moon’s glow. The villagers, once skeptical, now looked at her with a newfound respect.

But Seraphina knew the truth. Days before, she had discovered the puzzle’s secret mechanism by chance while exploring. Her success at the festival was not just a display of intellect but also of her cunning and ability to seize opportunities.

As the festival ended and the caravans prepared to leave Willowshade, the story of the young tiefling girl who unlocked the Whispering Willow spread like wildfire. Seraphina, sitting in a wagon, looked back at the hamlet, a sly grin on her lips. She had added another tale to her growing legend, another step in her journey through the lands of Mirrormere.

For Seraphina, each adventure was a thread in the intricate tapestry of her destiny, weaving her path towards a future where her cunning and charm would become the stuff of legend.

APPENDIX F — Flash Fiction

Title: The Shadow of the Silent Bell

In the heart of Mirrormere, shrouded in the mists of enigma, lay the village of Silentbell. Known for its ancient, voiceless bell tower, a structure steeped in legend, Silentbell was a place where tales of magic and mystery lingered in the air like the morning fog. Here, among the cobblestone streets and ivy-clad walls, Seraphina, now nine, found herself on the cusp of a defining moment.

The Silent Bell, as the villagers called it, had not rung for centuries. Legend held that only a soul of purest intent could awaken its slumbering chime. Each year, the village held a festival where children tried to ring the bell, their laughter and cheers echoing through the streets. Yet, the bell remained silent, its voice a forgotten whisper.

Seraphina, with her sharp mind and adventurous heart, was intrigued by the challenge. The night before the festival, under a blanket of stars, she crept to the bell tower. Her heart beat with a mix of excitement and a strange, unspoken apprehension.

Climbing the spiraling stairs, Seraphina reached the top, where the massive, dormant bell hung. Its surface was etched with symbols and runes, faintly glowing in the moonlight. Seraphina, running her fingers over the cold metal, felt a surge of energy, a connection that sent a shiver down her spine.

“Only a soul of purest intent…” she whispered, her thoughts a whirlwind of past pranks and mischief. Was she pure of intent? Did she even want to be?

With a deep breath, Seraphina reached for the bell’s rope. Her hands trembled, not with fear, but with the weight of a choice. Ringing this bell, awakening a legend — it was a moment of truth, a mirror to her soul.

The village, asleep and unknowing, lay spread out below her. Seraphina could be the hero, the child of legend who awakened the Silent Bell. Or she could remain the trickster, the rogue, dancing on the edge of shadows.

The rope felt heavy in her hands, heavier than it should. It was not just a rope; it was a decision, a path.

With a surge of resolve, Seraphina pulled the rope. The bell stirred, a deep, resonant hum filling the air, but no chime followed. The bell remained silent, its voice still a memory.

Disappointed, Seraphina descended the tower as the first light of dawn painted the sky. The festival would go on, and she would watch, her secret safe.

But in her heart, a question lingered, echoing like the silent chime of the bell. Was the bell truly silent, or was it a reflection of the turmoil within her? In that moment, under the watchful eyes of the ancient bell, Seraphina stood at the crossroads of her destiny, the shadow of good and evil playing upon her soul.
