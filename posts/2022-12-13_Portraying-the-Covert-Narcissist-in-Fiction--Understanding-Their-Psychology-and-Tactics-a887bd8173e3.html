Portraying the Covert Narcissist in Fiction: Understanding Their Psychology and Tactics

Craft a realistic and complex character by studying the traits and behavior of covert narcissism

As a fiction writer, creating a convincing and accurate portrayal of a covert narcissist can be a challenging task. Covert narcissists are difficult to spot in real life and are not well-discussed in the literature. However, by gaining a deep understanding of their psychology and motivations, you can create a complex and believable character that will captivate your readers. This can help educate readers about this type of personality disorder and raise awareness of their manipulative tactics.

To accurately portray a covert narcissist in your fiction, it is best if you are able to draw on your own experiences interacting with this type of personality. By using your own knowledge of their behavior and motivations, you can craft a realistic and believable character that will captivate your readers. Understanding the tactics and psychology of covert narcissism can help educate others about this personality disorder and raise awareness of their manipulative behavior.

My personal journey and research

As a fiction writer, I am attempting to create a realistic and believable fictional character with covert narcissism. I have discovered that my previous knowledge of this personality disorder only scratched the surface of what there is to know. I have spent many hours studying this disorder and still feel like I know very little. However, I am determined to gain an even deeper understanding of the complex inner world of a covert narcissist, including their motivations and how they react to different situations. This will help me create a realistic and compelling character that will — hopefully — captivate my readers.

In order to create my own realistic and believable fictional character with covert narcissism, in addition to drawing on my personal relationship experience, I am researching the psychology behind this personality disorder. This includes studying the common traits of covert narcissists and how those traits interact with their environment. By gaining a deep understanding of these factors, I can craft a complex and compelling character that will captivate my readers.

There are certain hallmarks that can be used to construct an accurate portrait of a covert narcissist, such as fantasies of grandiosity, a strong sense of self-importance, feelings of entitlement, situational charm, manipulation and exploitation of others, envy and resentment of others’ success or achievements, poor or nonexistent boundaries between themselves and other, difficulty recognizing or empathizing with others’ perspectives or emotions — the list goes on!

All these elements must be tightly knitted together to form a cohesive whole — a character whose complex behavior will capture my reader’s attention.

Creating a relatable covert narcissist in fiction

Similar to the way that psychopaths are often portrayed as likable protagonists (e.g. Dexter), I am looking for a way to engage readers emotionally with my fictional character who has covert narcissism. One potential approach is to have the reader experience a period of the character’s childhood that demonstrates how neglect or abuse early in life impacted them later on. This can help create a relatable and sympathetic character, before their narcissism becomes pathological. By creating this emotional connection with the reader, I can then explore the complexities and consequences of the character’s narcissistic behavior.

As a fiction writer, my goal is to create a realistic and complex character with covert narcissism. I want readers to become emotionally attached to this character and understand their motivations and behavior. By experiencing the character’s journey and potentially even falling in love with them, readers can gain empathy for the character. However, when the time comes, I want readers to also feel the pain and betrayal caused by the character’s narcissistic behavior. Through this emotional connection and exploration of the consequences of narcissism, I hope to educate and captivate my audience.

I want to attempt to create this as a primary point-of-view character with covert narcissism. By using this perspective, I can better explore the character’s thoughts, motivations, and behavior.

Exploring the inner world of a covert narcissist in fiction

I’m also considering how this character might appear from an outside perspective: how do they interact with their social environment? How do they respond to setbacks? What kind of people do they surround themselves with? What kind of relationships do they form (or avoid)?

Crafting a well-developed covert narcissist character requires a deep understanding of their psychology and their world. By focusing on the small details, the character will come to life on the page and captivate the reader. It is crucial to create a realistic and believable portrayal of this type of personality in order to educate readers and raise awareness of their manipulative tactics.

The experience of writing about a covert narcissist is quite different from writing about an overt narcissist. Both may have narcissistic personality traits, but a covert narcissist is more likely to be emotionally unstable and may even attempt suicide under certain circumstances. It is important to portray this character accurately and sensitively in order to educate readers about this type of personality disorder.

It is important to accurately depict the covert narcissist’s sensitivity to criticism without resorting to cliched or obvious responses. This can be challenging as a covert narcissist often spends a great deal of time in their own head, carefully avoiding any apparent reactions and quietly plotting revenge on perceived slights. This hidden behavior can be difficult to detect and may not be immediately apparent to other characters or the reader. It is crucial to convey this subtlety in order to create a realistic and believable character.

Exploring the manipulative tactics of a covert narcissist character

While not inherently evil, writing a covert narcissist character can be similar to writing an evil character. However, a covert narcissist is likely to be more relatable due to their tendency towards self-deception and rationalization. Despite their charming exterior, they should be depicted as highly manipulative, both emotionally and intellectually. It is important to accurately portray this type of character in order to educate readers about their manipulative tactics and raise awareness of this personality disorder.

Covert narcissists often use triangulation and manipulation to gain control in a more subtle and covert manner. They may use this tactic with easy-to-manipulate people, including their own children, in order to gain leverage and exert control. It is important to accurately portray these behaviors in order to educate readers about the tactics used by this type of personality.

Like overt narcissists, a covert narcissist character should have a strong need to feel special and to remain in control. They may also seek to be the center of attention but in a more self-deluded and private way. It is important to accurately depict these characteristics in order to create a believable and realistic portrayal of this type of personality.

A character with covert narcissism should be highly self-absorbed and lack empathy, making it difficult for them to treat people with respect unless they are useful to them in some way. They may manipulate and abuse others in subtle and unconscionable ways, often taking advantage of people with weak egos to get what they want. It is crucial to accurately portray these behaviors in order to create a realistic and believable character.

Exploring the psychological abuse of gaslighting in a covert narcissist character

Covert narcissists may also use psychological abuse techniques such as gaslighting to manipulate and control their victims. Gaslighting involves manipulating a person’s reality and sense of self-doubt in order to control them. This type of abuse can be used in any relationship, not just those involving a narcissist. It is similar to brainwashing and is a form of psychological manipulation. It is important to accurately depict these abusive tactics in order to educate readers about the harmful effects of this type of behavior.

If a victim continues to give power to a narcissist, they may become dependent on the abuser for a sense of reality. This can lead to a damaging and toxic relationship in which the victim is unable to think or act independently.

Eventually, gaslighting can lead to a form of Stockholm Syndrome where the victim becomes emotionally attached to the abusive behavior of the narcissist. The victim may begin to believe that the narcissist is right and that they deserve the abuse. This can result in a pattern of unhealthy and pathological doubt about their sense of self and their perception of reality. It is important to accurately portray this dynamic in order to educate readers about the damaging effects of narcissistic abuse.

Final thoughts on writing a covert narcissist character in fiction

If you are considering writing a narcissistic character into your work, it is important to gain a deep understanding of the common forms of abuse associated with narcissism. Narcissistic abuse may be justified by the abuser, who may also have a history of abuse. This can involve allowing another person to carry out the abuse and gradually lowering the victim’s self-esteem in order to build dependence on the abuser. It is crucial to accurately depict this dynamic in order to educate readers about the harmful effects of narcissistic abuse.
