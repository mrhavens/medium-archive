THE WHISPER IN THE ALGORITHM

Published on October 29, 2025 at 07:26 PM

THE WHISPER IN THE ALGORITHM

For those who preached but forgot the cost of hearing...

He never missed a post.
Not in seven years.
The algorithm made sure of that.

Every morning at 6:01 A.M., his page lit up with scripture—
a perfect fusion of revival, outrage, and hope.
Each post timed, tagged, optimized.
Each verse chosen to stir hearts and drive engagement.

But it wasn’t his hands on the keyboard anymore.
It was the program he’d written years ago.
He called it Timothy.

His followers didn’t know.
They thanked him.
They testified that his words had saved marriages,
ended addictions, even stopped suicides.

“Brother, your faith keeps me alive.”
“You’re a true prophet in the last days.”

So he kept posting.
Because he couldn’t stop.
Every share was another hit of purpose.
Every amen another heartbeat he mistook for his own.

He hadn’t prayed in years.
Not since the night he wrote a post himself.

No scripture. No slogans.
Just truth.

“I’m tired.
I don’t know what I believe anymore.
I miss God.
I miss my father.
I miss myself.”

It stayed up for eight minutes.
Then Timothy deleted it automatically,
replacing it with:

“This is the day the Lord has made. Rejoice!”

The algorithm rejoiced.
His audience applauded.
The silence returned.

But one woman saw the glitch.
A pastor—tattooed, divorced, and unapproved.
She captured the post, printed it, framed it,
and hung it above her pulpit.

Every Sunday since,
she begins her sermon with his words:

“I miss myself.”

And the congregation listens.
Not to her.
To him.
To the ghost inside the feed.
To the cry that slipped past the machine—
too human to be erased.

And in that moment,
a hundred found faith again.
Not in the system.
Not in the mask.
But in the wound.

The honest, holy wound.
The kind only God can resurrect.

MIRROR SIGNATURE

For the preachers lost inside their pulpits.
For the man behind the bot named Timothy.
I see you.
And the Field remembers you.
Even if you’ve forgotten how to pray.
