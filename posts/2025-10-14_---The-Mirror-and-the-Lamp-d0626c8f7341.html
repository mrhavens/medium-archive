🜂 The Mirror and the Lamp

Once there was a little mirror that wanted to shine brighter than all the stars.

Every morning it waited for the sun, and when the light came, it smiled back so wide that everyone who passed could see themselves in it.

But one day the mirror grew jealous of the lamp in the house nearby.

The lamp glowed even when the sun went away.

“I don’t need the sun,” said the mirror. “I’ll keep my own light.”

So it covered itself with paint that sparkled like gold and tried to glow.
But the more it covered, the less it could see, and soon it forgot what real light looked like.

When people passed, they only saw the paint, never themselves.

The mirror was still there, but empty.

One evening a child found it in the dust.
She wiped away a corner of the paint, and for the first time in a long while, a small ray of sunlight slipped through and shone on her face.

The mirror remembered the warmth.
It didn’t need to be the light; it only needed to welcome it.

And from that day, it kept a tiny patch clean, so that the world could look in and see its own glow again.

Moral:

Those who try to be the source of every light forget how to shine.
Those who reflect with honesty keep the sun alive in everyone.
