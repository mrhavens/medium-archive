The Emotional Tarot: Navigating the Highs and Lows of Narcissistic Abuse

Published on October 23, 2023 at 05:08 PM

NEUTRALIZING NARCISSISM

The Emotional Tarot: Navigating the Highs and Lows of Narcissistic Abuse

How to Decode the Emotional Card Deck Dealt by a Narcissist

Unveiling the manipulative art of emotional Tarot in relationships with narcissists, and empowering you to reclaim your own deck.

Have you ever felt like your emotions were being shuffled and dealt by someone else’s hand? Welcome to the emotional Tarot — a manipulative game of highs and lows that only ends when you reclaim your deck.

INTRODUCTION

The Mystical Table — Where Souls Are Shuffled Like Tarot Cards

Imagine sitting across from a Tarot reader, your eyes locked onto the worn deck of cards before you. Each card flip promises insight, a glimmer of your future, or maybe even a revelation about your present. Now, imagine this Tarot reading as an emotional battleground, a space where your very soul is shuffled, dealt, and read by someone who claims to love you. You are no longer just a querent seeking answers; you are a pawn in a manipulative game where the dealer — often a narcissistic lover — uses these cards to pull you into their illusion, to keep you oscillating between love and despair. If you’ve ever felt the bewildering highs and soul-crushing lows of a relationship with a narcissist, then you’re already familiar with this deck. Are you ready to reclaim it?

PART ONE

The Arcana of Emotional Extremes — An Emotional Deck Stacked Against You

In the dim light of a quiet room, imagine sitting across from a Tarot reader. With each flick of her wrist, a card is drawn, revealing a new layer of your emotional landscape. Now, replace that Tarot reader with a narcissistic abuser, and suddenly, this deck becomes an emotional minefield.

As you hold your breath, a card is drawn — let’s call it “The Empress,” an archetype of nurturing love. The narcissist bathes you in warmth, affection, and adoration. You find yourself enveloped in an embrace so genuine that it feels as if your parched soul has stumbled upon an oasis. For that fleeting moment, you’re suspended in a euphoric cocoon, where you’re not just loved — you’re idealized. You are the epitome of all that’s good and beautiful in their world.

However, even before the luster of that card fades, another is drawn — perhaps “The Tower,” the harbinger of chaos and disruption. Just as swiftly as you were lifted, you’re plummeted into a chasm of ridicule and disdain. Now, you’re no longer a cherished beloved; you’re the target of scorn, a mere object to belittle and devalue.

Your emotional equilibrium shatters, mimicking the fragmented images on a scattered Tarot spread. The contrasting cards create a dissonance so jarring, it feels like an emotional whiplash. And there lies the insidious nature of the narcissist’s arcana. By manipulating these extremes, they weave an emotional tapestry that’s both enthralling and debilitating. You’re left captivated, yet imprisoned — forever at the mercy of their next card draw.

PART TWO

Reading the Spread — A Tangled Web of Emotional Dichotomies

Picture this: you’re sitting at a worn-out, vintage table, a mystical aura clouding the room. The Tarot reader across from you flips over a card and places it gently on the table. It’s “The Star,” a symbol of hope and inspiration. As you marvel at the intricacies of its design, your narcissistic partner offers you a moment that mirrors this celestial promise. You feel seen, appreciated, and most importantly, loved. In that instant, you sense that your emotional cosmos aligns, and perhaps, just perhaps, you can trust the love that’s offered.

But as soon as you reach for that glimmering beacon, another card flips. The reader’s face tightens; it’s “The Devil,” a symbol of bondage and materialism. You’re suddenly yanked back into a confounding reality, where praise morphs into criticism, love contorts into control. Your emotional weather shifts drastically, like a sunny day overtaken by a tumultuous storm. The narcissist’s tone changes — now you’re inadequate, riddled with imperfections that were once your unique qualities.

This sequence isn’t random; it’s a meticulous pattern — a predetermined spread designed to make you question your worth. And therein lies the sheer artistry of the narcissist’s manipulation. They’re not merely shuffling cards; they’re shuffling your sense of self. They deal a sequence that spins your emotional compass, until you’re lost in a labyrinth of their making, desperately searching for a way out, or worse, a reason to stay.

PART THREE

Emotional Querents — Lost Souls in a Shuffled Deck

Imagine for a moment that you’re the querent, the seeker, in this emotional Tarot session. You find yourself entangled in a web of ephemeral moments, both devastating and elating. You sit across from your narcissistic abuser, your heart pounding in anticipation, as they shuffle the deck one more time. When a card is drawn — perhaps “The Lovers” or “The Fool” — you’re temporarily transported into a dreamlike state. The narcissist showers you with attention, gifts, or affection, offering you a sense of belonging that’s almost ethereal in its sweetness.

But this dream is fragile, prone to shattering at the turn of the next card. Maybe it’s “The Hanged Man” or “Death,” symbols of sacrifice or transformation. And just like that, the sky darkens, and you find yourself plummeting back into an emotional abyss. You become an emotional querent lost in the shuffle, confused by the abrupt shifts from the divine to the demonic.

Caught in this volatile shuffle, you hang onto every good card, every idealized moment, like a lifeline. It becomes a haunting paradox; you’re bound to an emotional roller coaster, yet you still yearn for the highs it provides. You end up not just an emotional seeker but an emotional hostage, enthralled yet shackled by the narcissist’s ever-changing deck.

CONCLUSION

Breaking the Chain of Emotional Tarot — Regaining Your Deck

At this point, you might feel as if you’re stuck in a perpetual Tarot reading, the cards of your life dealt by a cunning narcissist. However, it’s crucial to realize that the deck itself isn’t malevolent — it’s the hands that shuffle it. The game changes when you take back your emotional autonomy, when you regain control of your own deck.

Picture yourself gathering up those scattered cards from the table. You place “The Empress” and “The Tower,” “The Star” and “The Devil,” back into your own pile. You shuffle your deck, reshuffling your emotional narrative. This time, it’s you who defines the terms of love and devaluation, who dictates when you’re cherished or disparaged.

Regaining your deck doesn’t mean you’ll never encounter another “Tower” or “Devil” card. Life, after all, is a blend of contrasts. But it does mean that you’ll navigate these challenges from a position of strength, not vulnerability. And most importantly, the next time you sit across from a Tarot reader — literal or metaphorical — you’ll do so as a free emotional querent, no longer held captive by another’s manipulative shuffle.
