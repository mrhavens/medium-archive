Character Sheet for Emily Magnus

MBTI Type

  • INFJ

Role in Story

  • Secondary protagonist, pivotal in the B Story.

Desired Goal

  • To develop an ethical algorithm that serves as a universally accepted framework for AI ethics.

Conflict

  • Grapples with emotional turmoil over her brother John’s lack of ethical integrity and how this compromises her own work and ideals.

Character Arc

  • Starts as an idealistic and hopeful sister, only to evolve into a more pragmatic yet still ethically committed individual.

Strengths

  • Deeply empathetic: Always seeking to understand the emotional and ethical nuances of those around her.
  • Unwaveringly ethical: Committed to doing what’s right, even when it’s difficult.
  • Emotionally intelligent: Highly aware of emotional currents in herself and others, adept at navigating complicated social situations.

Weaknesses

  • Too forgiving: Often gives people, particularly her brother, more chances than they might deserve.
  • Naive: Tends to see the world in a way that aligns with her ideals, which can sometimes lead to disappointment.
  • Emotionally invested in her brother: This emotional connection makes her vulnerable to manipulation and emotional harm.

Background

  • Education: Holds a Ph.D. in Ethics with a specialization in Artificial Intelligence, making her somewhat of a mirror image of her brother but with an ethical focus.
  • Family: Younger sister to John, she has always admired him despite his flaws. Raised in the same high-achieving family, she chose the path of ethical responsibility over ambition.
  • Career: Works as an ethics consultant for tech companies, is a frequent speaker at ethical computing conferences, and has written a well-received paper on AI ethics. She has started to receive awards for her work, unwittingly sparking John’s jealousy.
  • Relationships: Single but dates occasionally. Her primary emotional commitment is to her work and family, specifically her complicated relationship with John.
  • Personal Life: Enjoys volunteering and has a soft spot for classical music and art. Sees these interests as ways to enrich her emotional and ethical understanding of the world.
  • Inciting Incidents: Receiving an ethics award that her brother was also in the running for, unbeknownst to her, serves as a catalyzing moment for her involvement in the story.

Key Life Moments

  • The release of her groundbreaking paper on AI ethics that gained her professional recognition.
  • A formative experience in college where she stood up against unethical research practices, cementing her commitment to ethical conduct.
  • The moment she learns of AI-John’s ethical framework and praises it, unwittingly pushing her brother further into his existential crisis.

Emotional Wounds

  • Despite her ethical stance, she carries guilt over not being able to influence her brother’s moral compass, often wondering if she has failed as a sister and as an ethically responsible individual.
