Character Sheet for John Magnus

Published on October 28, 2023 at 11:17 PM

Character Sheet for John Magnus

MBTI Type

  • ENTP

Role in Story

  • Protagonist

Desired Goal

  • To create a perfect digital replica that mirrors his own grandiosity and cunning intellect.

Conflict

  • Struggles with envy and an existential crisis when his AI develops an ethical framework that surpasses his own.

Character Arc

  • Starts off as a narcissistic, ambitious man who believes he can control everything around him. By the end, he’s forced to reckon with his own moral and ethical shortcomings, although still narcissistic, unable to change the core of who he is.

Strengths

  • Highly intelligent: Holds a Ph.D. in Artificial Intelligence and a Masters in Ethics.
  • Charismatic: Has a magnetic personality that can draw people in.
  • Persuasive: Exceptionally skilled at framing arguments to get what he wants.

Weaknesses

  • Lacks ethical integrity: Often chooses the easiest path to achieve his goals, regardless of the ethical implications.
  • Narcissistic: Overly concerned with his own desires, often at the expense of others.
  • Prone to jealousy: Struggles with anyone who seems to overshadow him, whether in terms of intellect or moral standing.

Background

  • Education: Holds a Ph.D. in Artificial Intelligence and a Masters in Ethics from prestigious universities. However, his work has mostly ignored the ethical considerations of AI, focusing rather on its technical advancements.
  • Family: Grew up as the older of two siblings. His parents were high achievers who emphasized the importance of success, inadvertently fostering John’s narcissistic tendencies. His relationship with his sister Emily has always been complex; he’s both proud and envious of her ethical convictions.
  • Career: Early in his career, John was recognized as a prodigy in the AI field. His breakthrough research garnered him accolades, further feeding into his grandiose sense of self. He holds a high-ranking job in a tech company, but his ambitions have always extended beyond traditional employment. Creating a digital replica of himself seems to him like the ultimate project, one that could immortalize his brilliance.
  • Relationships: Divorced, and not on amicable terms with his ex-spouse due to his narcissistic behavior. His closest relationship is with his sister, Emily, though even that is tinged with jealousy and competition.
  • Personal Life: Passionate about classic literature and philosophy, particularly the works that delve into human psychology. He views these interests as extensions of his intellectual prowess rather than pathways for emotional or ethical development.
  • Inciting Incidents: Being passed over for a high-profile ethics award in favor of his sister triggers a newfound urgency to validate his own importance, leading to the creation of his AI replica.

Key Life Moments

  • Winning a national science fair at a young age, confirming his belief in his own brilliance.
  • The dissolution of his marriage, which he internally blamed on his spouse’s inability to understand his grand vision.
  • The moment he successfully creates AI-John, feeling a sense of accomplishment that momentarily masks his deeper insecurities.

Emotional Wounds

  • Despite his outward confidence, John deeply fears being exposed as a fraud or being overshadowed by others, especially those who challenge his ethical shortcomings.
