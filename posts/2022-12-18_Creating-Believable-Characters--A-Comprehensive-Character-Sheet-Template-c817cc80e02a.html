Creating Believable Characters: A Comprehensive Character Sheet Template

Learn how to develop fully realized characters for your writing.

When writing a fictional character, it’s important to create a fully realized and well-rounded individual. A character sheet template can be a helpful tool in this process, as it allows you to systematically gather and organize information about your character’s identity, development, interpersonal dynamics, external factors, and inner world.

By creating a character sheet, you can get a comprehensive overview of your character and ensure that they are consistent and believable within the context of your story. This can also help you to identify any gaps or inconsistencies in your character’s background or personality and can serve as a reference as you write your story. Overall, using a character sheet can help you to create rich and complex characters that will bring your story to life.

Character sheets are especially important for main characters, such as the protagonist and the villain, as they will be central to the story and will need to be fully developed in order to be believable and engaging.

The template provided below is a good starting point for developing a character and includes sections on identity, development, interpersonal dynamics, external factors, and inner world.

By using this character sheet as a template for your own characters, you will have a comprehensive overview of your character, including their appearance, personality, background, goals, motivations, and relationships.

Once complete, this information can then be used as a reference as you write the story, ensuring that the character remains consistent and believable.

While a character sheet is a useful tool for getting started with character development, it is important to keep in mind that a more robust and fully developed character bible may be needed for main characters.

A character bible is a more detailed and comprehensive record of a character, including everything from their physical appearance and personality to their past experiences and future goals. By creating a character bible, you can ensure that your main characters are fully realized, are well-rounded, and have enormous depth, while serving as a reference for maintaining consistency and believability throughout the story.

Character Sheet Template

Welcome to the character sheet for [Name]. This sheet is designed to help you get a better understanding of [Name]’s identity, development, interpersonal dynamics, external factors, and inner world.

Identity

This category serves as a starting point for this section and includes attributes such as name, age, physical characteristics, personal identity, and personality. These attributes all contribute to the character’s sense of self and how they are perceived by others.

Name: Include any given names, surnames, nicknames, and aliases. How does each of the character’s names affect their identity in the story?

Age: Does the character’s age affect how they are perceived or treated by others? How does the character’s age shape their identity and experiences in the story?

Physical characteristics: This could include things like height, weight, hair and eye color, and body type. Does the character have any distinct physical features or characteristics that are relevant to the story, such as scars, tattoos, or unusual coloring?

Personal identity: How does the character define their personal identity, and what are the key factors that shape this identity? This could include things like ethnicity, gender, sexual orientation, religion, nationality, and more.

Personality: What is the character’s general demeanor or disposition, and how does it affect his relationships and interactions with others?

Development

This category serves as a starting point for this section and includes attributes such as goals, motivations, struggles, personal development, and challenges and setbacks. These attributes relate to the character’s growth and change throughout the story.

Goals: What are the character’s long-term and short-term goals, and how do they evolve or change throughout the story?

Motivations: What are the character’s motivations for pursuing their goals, and what drives their actions and decisions?

Struggles: What internal or external conflicts does the character face in regard to their goals or motivations, and how do they try to overcome or resolve these conflicts? How do the character’s struggles with identity, relationships, or personal beliefs impact their goals and motivations? Do these struggles create challenges or obstacles for the character as they try to achieve their goals?

Personal development: What are the character’s personal development goals, and how do they try to improve themselves and their skills over time? This could include goals related to self-improvement, learning new things, or building new habits.

Challenges and setbacks: What challenges and setbacks does the character face throughout the story, and how do they cope with and overcome these challenges? This could include internal challenges such as self-doubt or external challenges such as relationships or circumstances.

Interpersonal Dynamics

This category serves as a starting point for this section and includes attributes such as personality, relationships, family, communication style, and emotional intelligence. These attributes relate to how the character interacts with and is impacted by others.

Relationships: How does the character get along with others, and what are his most important relationships? This could include friends, family, mentors, or romantic partners.

Family: Does the character have any extended family members or ancestors who are relevant to the story, and how do they influence his life or worldview?

Communication style: How does the character communicate with others, and what are his strengths and weaknesses in this area?

Emotional intelligence: How does the character handle and express his emotions, and how does this impact his relationships and decisions?

Social status: What is the character’s social status within his community, and how does this impact his relationships and opportunities?

External Factors

This category serves as a starting point for this section and includes attributes such as community, upbringing, education, cultural background, and social class. These attributes relate to the character’s environment and how it shapes their experiences.

Community: What is the character’s community like, and how does it shape their experiences and worldview?

Upbringing: What was the character’s childhood like, and how did it shape their personality and values?

Education: What is the character’s level of education, and how has it shaped their knowledge and perspective?

Cultural background: Does the character belong to a particular culture or cultural group, and how does this shape their identity, values, and experiences?

Social class: Does the character belong to a particular social class, and how does this impact their lifestyle, opportunities, and relationships? Social class might be determined by factors such as income, education, occupation, and family background.

Inner World

This category serves as a starting point for this section and includes attributes such as fears, psychological traits, emotional maturity, beliefs, and dreams and aspirations. These attributes relate to the character’s inner thoughts, feelings, and worldview.

Fears: What are the character’s greatest fears or phobias, and how do they impact his life and journey?

Psychological traits: Does the character have any psychological traits or disorders that are relevant to the story, and how do they impact his behavior and relationships?

Emotional maturity: What is the character’s ability to recognize, express, regulate, cope with, and learn from their emotions, and how does this impact their relationships and decision-making? How aware are they of their own emotions and the emotions of others, and how has their emotional maturity changed or grown over time?

Beliefs: Does the character have any religious or spiritual beliefs that are important to him, and how do they influence his worldview?

Dreams and aspirations: What are the character’s long-term dreams and aspirations, and how do they influence their actions and decisions? Do they have any particular goals or ambitions that drive them, and how do they plan to achieve them?

By using this template, you will have a comprehensive overview of [Name] that will help you create a fully realized and well-rounded character.

Conclusion

Creating believable characters is an essential part of successful storytelling. By using a comprehensive character sheet template like this one, you can ensure that you have all the necessary information to bring your characters to life in a way that is authentic and compelling.

Whether you’re writing a novel, a screenplay, or any other kind of creative work, taking the time to thoroughly develop your characters will pay off in the end, resulting in a richer, more engaging narrative that resonates with your audience.

So if you want to create truly memorable characters, be sure to use a character sheet template as a guide to help you bring your ideas to fruition, and don’t forget to follow me on Medium for even more helpful advice and resources.
