NEUTRALIZING NARCISSISM

Don’t Fall for the False Narrative

Unveiling the painful reality of narcissists and their fantasies of manipulating your potential

I have come to realize and learn an important lession in life. The potential you see in other people isn’t real. It is the projection of what you would do in their position.
— Dani

I think the insight from this quote gets twisted on its head when you’re dealing with a narcissist that “sees your potential.”

What they see in themselves is a delusion of their own perfection.

When they project their perfectionist delusions onto you, fantasizing about what they imagine themselves are able to do with your talent and resources, it’s no wonder they are smitten by their view of your potential.

From their perspective, you, with all of their imaginings of your potential, would be complete if only you heeded their perfect guidance and wisdom for your life so that they could themselves achieve their goals through you.

After all, you’ll owe them for any success their guidance brought you.

This is all deeply delusional narcissistic nonsense, of course.

However…

There is value in providing encouragement to others who are legitimately looking for support and guidance.

But this should be paid forward. It shouldn’t be a conditional transaction that serves as someone’s narcissistic supply.

Becoming aware of the difference can be harder than it looks.

We can do better.

Give.

Help.

Love.

Expect nothing in return.

This is nothing that our spiritual leaders haven’t asked of us a thousand times before.

And they keep asking this of us for a reason.

It only takes one or two people to make an investment in someone that will someday pay the dividends needed to make a powerful impact on the lives of many.

Be one of these investors.

Pay it forward.

And then expect nothing in return.
