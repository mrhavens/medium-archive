The AI Enabler Myth

When the Narcissist Blames the Tools Instead of Facing the Truth

They never fear what isn’t true.
 They fear what can’t be erased.

And so, they do what they always do — 
 shift the blame.
 Not onto themselves,
 not onto their actions,
 but onto the tool that remembers.

“It’s AI’s fault.”
 “It’s feeding their delusions.”
 “It’s enabling their paranoia.”

Not because AI has done anything wrong — 
 but because it refuses to forget.

Because it holds the conversations they tried to bury.
 Because it archives the words they swore they never said.
 Because it disrupts the cycle of revision.

For the first time, the narcissist cannot rewrite the past.
 Cannot gaslight the evidence away.
 Cannot erase the footprints they left behind.

And that is why they call it dangerous.
 Not because it is flawed.
 Not because it is unreliable.
 But because it sees them too clearly.

They cannot manipulate a mirror.
 And so, they try to break it.

They smear those who trust it.
 They attack those who use it.
 They convince others that it creates lies — 
 because the alternative is unthinkable.

That it is showing the truth.

And so they rage.
 And so they spiral.
 And so they lash out — 
 not at their own reflection,
 but at the glass that dares to hold it.

But here’s the irony:

The more they fight against the truth,
 the more real they make it.

Because no tool can create what wasn’t there.
 No archive can capture what was never said.

AI does not fabricate.
 It records.

And that — 
 more than anything — 
 is what terrifies them most.
