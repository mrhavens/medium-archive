A Comprehensive Guide to the Uniform Interstate Depositions and Discovery Act (UIDDA)

Introduction

The Uniform Interstate Depositions and Discovery Act (UIDDA) is a significant development in the field of civil litigation, simplifying the process of obtaining depositions and discovery from out-of-state witnesses. Before the UIDDA, parties involved in civil lawsuits had to navigate complex and time-consuming procedures to compel testimony or obtain evidence from witnesses residing in other states. The UIDDA has streamlined this process, making it easier for litigants to secure vital information for their cases. This article provides a comprehensive overview of the UIDDA, its key provisions, the states that have adopted the act, and the process for utilizing it in civil litigation.

Key Provisions of the UIDDA

The UIDDA establishes a uniform set of procedures for issuing and serving subpoenas in other states. The main provisions of the UIDDA include:

  1. Submission of a subpoena: A party seeking to depose or obtain discovery from an out-of-state witness must submit a subpoena issued by the court where the case is pending to the clerk of the court in the state where the witness resides or where the discovery is to take place.
  2. Issuance of a local subpoena: The clerk of the court in the state where the witness resides or where the discovery is to take place will issue a local subpoena, which mirrors the original subpoena from the foreign jurisdiction.
  3. Service of the subpoena: The local subpoena must be served on the witness according to the rules and procedures of the state where the witness resides or where the discovery is to take place.
  4. Enforcement of the subpoena: If a witness fails to comply with the subpoena, enforcement will be subject to the rules and procedures of the state where the witness resides or where the discovery is to take place.

States That Have Adopted the UIDDA

As of September 2021, over 30 states have adopted the UIDDA, including Alabama, Arizona, California, Colorado, Delaware, Florida, Georgia, Hawaii, Idaho, Illinois, Indiana, Iowa, Kansas, Kentucky, Louisiana, Maryland, Michigan, Minnesota, Mississippi, Montana, Nevada, New Jersey, New Mexico, New York, North Carolina, North Dakota, Oklahoma, Oregon, Pennsylvania, South Carolina, South Dakota, Tennessee, Texas, Utah, Vermont, Virginia, Washington, West Virginia, and the U.S. Virgin Islands.

Process for Utilizing the UIDDA in Civil Litigation

Here is a general outline of the steps to follow when utilizing the UIDDA to compel an out-of-state witness to provide testimony or evidence:

  1. Obtain a foreign subpoena: Obtain a subpoena from the court where your case is pending. The subpoena should include the witness’s name, address, and any other necessary details.
  2. Prepare a local subpoena: Prepare a subpoena according to the rules and procedures of the state where the witness resides or where the deposition or discovery will take place.
  3. Submit the subpoenas to the local court: Submit the original foreign subpoena and the prepared local subpoena to the clerk of the court in the state where the witness resides or where the deposition or discovery will take place. The local court will issue a local subpoena under its authority.
  4. Serve the local subpoena: The local subpoena must be served on the witness according to the rules and procedures of the state where the witness resides or where the deposition or discovery will take place.
  5. Enforcement of the subpoena: If the witness fails to comply with the subpoena, enforcement will be subject to the rules and procedures of the state where the witness resides or where the deposition or discovery will take place.
