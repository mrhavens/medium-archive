OUTLINE & DRAFT

Reviving Your Narrative: A “Save the Cat!” Intervention

Resurrecting Stories from Disarray to Masterpiece

In the shadows of storytelling disarray, a writer harnesses the illuminating Save the Cat! method, transforming a fragmented tale into a riveting odyssey of camaraderie and adventure.
Delve into the transformative power of the Save the Cat! approach, a beacon for authors to turn disjointed tales into captivating narratives. Ever felt the weight of a promising but disjointed manuscript? Discover the magic of Save the Cat! and watch your narrative come to life.

Crafting the Heart: The Logline

Every story’s essence can be distilled into a single, captivating sentence: the logline. Our tale’s heartbeat resonated with:

An introverted seeker and a spirited young coachman, polar opposites in nature, confront otherworldly perils together.

This logline not only clarifies the story’s core but serves as a guiding star throughout the writing process.

From Blueprint to Framework

The Save the Cat! methodology offers a clear roadmap, pinpointing essential turning points that guide the narrative’s structure. Through this approach, we reorganized the initial scattered scenes, adjusting character motivations and event sequences. The outcome is a foundational narrative, establishing the groundwork for further depth and emotional resonance in subsequent revisions.

Laying the Foundation

Each scene was sketched out, capturing the essence while leaving room for further refinement. These initial outlines, though brief, brought our introspective hero’s journey to the forefront. The actions and dialogues, aligned with the story’s beats, provided a skeletal structure to the narrative. This foundational draft, while not final, sets the stage for deeper exploration and enhancement in subsequent revisions.

Journey of Transformation

For the inquisitive readers, the original narrative served as a commendable exercise in character development, shining with vibrant personalities yet lacking a cohesive plot. However, the magic of the “Save the Cat!” methodology has transformed this character-centric sketch. By infusing the “buddy love” genre and following the structured guidance, what follows is a first draft that evolves the initial character study into a tale brimming with companionship and adventure. ACT I

One

OPENING IMAGE

Darago and Sam begin their journey in the stagecoach, contrasting personalities established.

The jostling of the stagecoach grated on Darago’s senses as the journey to Ximes began. He stared pensively out the window, longing for stillness and quiet. His traveling companion, the young coach driver Sam, was the opposite — loud, energetic, chatting incessantly. Darago envied the boy’s carefree spirit, but such ceaseless interaction was exhausting.

“You built this yourself when you were three years old?” Darago responded drily to Sam’s latest repetitious tale. The claim seemed as absurd as the rest of Sam’s far-fetched stories. How could someone so young construct such a complex vehicle? Yet Darago held his skepticism in check. Sam’s unflappable enthusiasm was oddly endearing, like a puppy playfully nipping at your heels.

Darago sank back into his seat with a sigh, bracing for the long ride ahead. He knew Sam meant well, but the youth’s buoyant personality clashed with Darago’s introspective calm. Sam bubbled over with excitement for each new day, while Darago sought meaning and purpose on an inner journey within his rich inner world. Could they find common ground? Darago yearned to connect with the chatty coachman, to unite their contrasts into a smooth, peaceful balance. But first he had to reach Ximes and secure the potion vital to his personal quest. Darago could only hope this trip might lead to understanding, both of himself and of his brightly burning new companion.

Two

THEME STATED

Darago wants a quiet, peaceful ride while Sam enthusiastically engages him in conversation.

Darago gazed contemplatively out the jostling stagecoach as Sam prattled on enthusiastically. He felt an almost parental tenderness towards the boy’s tireless chatter, like a murmuring brook bubbling over scattered stones. Yet he longed for stillness, to dive deep into fathomless inner oceans.

Sam represented all that unsettled Darago — loud, energetic, always rushing on to the next new experience. Darago envied the childlike wonder Sam found in each moment. But Darago’s spirit sought something deeper — truths that could only be grasped in silence.

Still, Darago reminded himself, Sam was but an innocent. The boy meant no harm with his nonstop stories and grandiose claims. Darago almost smiled picturing this uncanny child building an entire stagecoach alone at the age of three. Clearly Sam felt driven to mythologize himself, to be seen as uniquely gifted. A mere desire for respect, or human connection, perhaps?

Darago knew well the longing to be truly seen and valued for who one is inside. Yet he had always struggled to properly convey his rich inner reality. Sam seemed to feel that struggle too, beneath the facade of carefree confidence. Darago realized how alike they were — two souls hoping to be appreciated for their gifts, yet going about it in wildly divergent ways.

Might they meet in the middle: Daragolearning from Sam’s easy outward confidence, and Sam drawn inwards to the stirring depths beneath the surface? As the stagecoach rolled on, Darago resolved to try bridging their differences. For true understanding came not by demanding others change, but by seeking the hidden places where even the most dissimilar minds might find harmony.

Three

SET-UP

Darago reveals he’s going to Ximes for an important potion quest. Sam warns about cannibalistic halflings.

The stagecoach hit a bump, jolting Darago from his contemplations. “So what brings you to Ximes anyway?” Sam asked. “It’s an odd destination for most folks.”

Darago hesitated before answering. He disliked revealing his personal affairs. But Sam had proven an amicable companion thus far. “I seek a rare potion,” Darago said at last. “It is essential for a, shall we say, personal quest of mine.”

“Ooh, mysterious!” Sam said, eyes glinting playfully. “Well I hope your quest succeeds, though Ximes can be a tricky place. Have you heard about the halflings there?”

Darago tensed. “No, what about them?”

“They seem cute and friendly, but turn brutal on occasion,” Sam said, lowering his voice dramatically. “Rumor has it, some of them devour tall folks like us! Bones and all!”

Darago paled, cursing his luck. Of course the one potion he needed was in a city of cannibalistic halflings. But he refused to be deterred from his journey’s purpose, so close to grasping what he sought. Darago took a deep breath, steadying his rattled nerves. He had overcome far worse than a few hungry little people.

“I appreciate the warning,” Darago told Sam solemnly. “But I cannot turn back now. I will just have to be cautious when dealing with these halflings you speak of.”

Sam nodded, seeming to appreciate Darago’s quiet courage. They rode on in thoughtful silence, each contemplating what lay ahead.

Four

CATALYST

The stagecoach breaks a wheel, forcing Sam and Darago to work together to fix it.

The stagecoach hit a deep rut in the road, jolting violently. Darago braced himself as the vehicle listed sharply to the left. Sam pulled hard on the reins, bringing them to a stop.

“Dang wheel’s broken!” Sam cried, jumping down to inspect it. The front left wheel had cracked and split right through the spokes.

Darago grimaced. He disliked delays, but saw no choice except to help. He joined Sam examining the broken wheel.

“No problem, we’ll just replace it with that spare in back!” Sam said cheerfully.

Together they worked to prop up the coach and remove the damaged wheel. As he turned the replacement into place, Darago marveled at Sam’s resilience. Even stranded on a remote road, the boy bubbled with optimism.

Working side by side, they soon had the new wheel installed. Darago tightened the last bolts, feeling satisfied. The mishap had brought them together, bonding through shared effort.

Sam beamed at their handiwork. “We sure fixed that fast, partner!” He playfully elbowed Darago, who managed a thin smile in response. Back on the road, their destination awaited.

Five

DEBATE

Stuck waiting for the wheel to be repaired, Darago and Sam debate their outlooks on life and working together.

Here’s a continuation of the scene with a debate between Darago and Sam:

With the broken wheel repaired, Darago climbed back into the stagecoach, eager to resume their journey. But Sam seemed in no hurry to leave.

“We make a pretty good team, eh?” he said, plopping down beside Darago. “With your calm head and my roguish charm, we could start our own stagecoach business!”

Darago stiffened. “A business partnership? I hardly think…”

“Oh come on, it’ll be fun!” Sam interrupted. “Just imagine — the open road, new vistas every day, meeting all kinds of folk…”

Darago held up a hand. “Those things delight you, but not I. Our dispositions differ greatly.”

Sam’s face fell momentarily before his enthusiasm resurged. “Opposites attract though! We balance each other out.”

Darago weighed Sam’s words. Perhaps the boy had a point. Still, he felt hesitant to bind their fates together.

Sensing his reluctance, Sam pressed on earnestly. “No need to decide now, just think it over. We may seem an odd match, but that’s why it works! Like sunshine and rain coming together to grow things.”

Darago managed a small smile. The child’s sparkling spirit indeed reminded him of sunbeams piercing clouds. “Let us see what this journey holds,” he conceded mildly. “Then perhaps we can discuss working together.”

Sam grinned and flicked the reins. An unlikely partnership seemed to be blossoming.

ACT II

Six

BREAK INTO ACT TWO

The repaired stagecoach gets moving again. Sam and Darago reach a new understanding.

The repaired wheel held steady as the stagecoach rolled onward. Darago felt his anxieties about this strange journey easing somewhat. Despite their differences, Sam had proven himself a capable guide thus far.

Darago gazed over at the boy coachman. Sam rode with a bright smile, as if simply being alive brought him joy. Darago envied such an untroubled spirit. Yet he saw now that beneath Sam’s playful ramblings lay hidden depth, like secrets murmuring through the babble of a brook.

“Thank you for your efforts earlier,” Darago remarked. “With the broken wheel. You handled it well.”

Sam beamed at the praise. “Couldn’t have done it without you! We’re a regular pair of blacksmiths, ain’t we?”

Darago chuckled softly. “Perhaps so. I confess I underestimated your maturity at first. But you bear burdens well for one so young.”

Sam puffed up proudly. “And you’re not as gloomy as I thought! Together we’ll make it through.”

The rocky trail smoothed out as they rolled on. Each now saw the other’s real worth. Traveling companions yesterday, today they were friends, ready to face the road ahead together.

Seven

B STORY

Sam opens up to Darago about his past and motivations for his upbeat attitude.

As the stagecoach ambled along, Sam’s chatter turned uncharacteristically introspective. “You ever wonder why I’m always yapping about adventures and such?” he asked Darago.

“I confess I have been curious,” Darago replied. “Your spirit seems eternally youthful.”

Sam sighed. “I wasn’t always this way. Ma and Pa got the fever when I was little. After that, my aunts raised me, and they were strict as teachers. I always had to sit still, stay quiet.”

Darago nodded, seeing Sam’s boundless energy in a new light. “So now you embrace each moment to the fullest.”

“Exactly! Being cooped up and proper, I felt like I couldn’t breathe.” Sam smiled wistfully. “But out here on the road, with the wind in my hair? For the first time I feel free.”

Darago regarded Sam thoughtfully. “Loss shapes us all,” he mused, “yet we each find our own path to healing.”

Sam’s face brightened with gratitude. “You really get it. Knew you would.”

Darago felt touched by Sam’s openness. This journey was teaching them more about themselves, and each other, than either had expected.

Eight

MIDPOINT

After bandits attack, Sam is injured protecting Darago, proving his loyalty.

The stagecoach was passing through a wooded valley when arrows thudded into the side of the coach. Bandits roared from the surrounding trees, emerging with swords drawn.

“Ambush!” Sam yelled, pulling hard on the reins to turn the coach around. But more brigands blocked the road ahead. They were trapped.

The bandit leader approached, grinning cruelly. “Your money or your lives!” he barked. Behind him, his gang had notched arrows aimed at Sam and Darago.

Darago quickly held up his coin purse, not wishing any harm to befall Sam. But as the bandit grabbed it, Sam suddenly lashed out with his whip. The attack startled the robbers, and Sam yelled “Go!” urgently to Darago.

Leaping from the coach, Darago fled into the woods. He heard fighting erupt behind him, but did not look back. Abruptly, an agonized scream in Sam’s voice pierced the air. Darago halted, torn. Should he keep running or try to help?

Before he could decide, the forest went quiet. Darago turned back to the road. The bandits were gone, and Sam lay crumpled beside the coach, bleeding from a gash on his side. Loyalty outweighing safety, he had risked his life for Darago.

Nine

BAD GUYS CLOSE IN

The stagecoach breaks down again near Ximes, and cannibalistic halflings start lurking.

Darago managed to bandage Sam’s wound and get him stabilized. Though still injured, Sam insisted on continuing their journey.

“We’re close now, I’ll be fine,” he said, gritting his teeth against the pain as he snapped the reins.

They traveled slowly the remaining distance to Ximes. But as the city came into view, the coach began to wobble. One of the rear wheels was splintering again.

The coach ground to a halt just outside the city gates. Darago assisted Sam down and went to inspect the broken wheel. This time it could not be repaired.

As Darago considered their options, he noticed stealthy movement in the bushes nearby. Small, furtive figures drew closer — the cannibalistic halflings Sam had warned about. They hissed and leered hungrily at the two stranded travelers.

Sam clung to Darago, face pale. “Thought we’d make it,” he whimpered through his pain. Darago’s heart sank. The journey that had bonded them now seemed likely to end in their deaths.

Ten

ALL IS LOST

The halflings capture Darago and prepare to cook him. Sam seems to have abandoned him.

The halflings crept nearer, making snarling noises as they eyed Darago and Sam. One grabbed a rock and hurled it, striking Darago’s leg. He cried out in pain as more rocks flew.

The halflings scurried forward and surrounded the stranded pair. Grabbing Darago’s arms, they tried to drag him away. He struggled furiously, but the strong little creatures overwhelmed him.

“Sam, run!” Darago shouted. But Sam just cowered against the coach, still too hurt to flee. Darago felt a pang of sorrow, seeing the spirited boy so defeated.

The halflings had no interest in the wounded Sam. They bound Darago’s hands and gagged him, then pulled him towards the city gates. Darago looked back desperately at Sam, but the boy seemed dazed, unaware of what was happening.

The halflings dragged Darago through the streets, leering up at him with hungry eyes. They took him to a grim stone building and locked him in a cell. Soon, the creatures returned wielding knives and pots.

“Put him in the stew, nice and slow,” their leader croaked. “Seasoning is the key.”

Horror flooded Darago. He was to be butchered like an animal, then eaten alive by these monsters. All his dreams would go unfulfilled.

Darago thought of Sam, wondering if the boy had regained himself and fled this awful place. Perhaps it was for the best that Darago faced his end alone. At least this way his friend might be spared.

With no hope left, Darago sank into despair. The halflings gleefully sharpened their blades, eager to begin carving their next meal. Darago closed his eyes, seeking some last shred of inner peace to cling to in his final moments. But he found only emptiness ahead of the void awaiting him.

Eleven

DARK NIGHT OF THE SOUL

Darago loses hope, accepting his doomed fate.

Locked in the dank cell, Darago felt all motivation drain away. He sat slumped against the cold stones as the halflings sharpened their knives just outside, chuckling eagerly about the meal to come.

Once Darago had burned with purpose, certain the potion he sought held the key to unlocking his destiny. But now, failure seemed his sole inheritance. Without that secret knowledge from Ximes, he would pass from this world unenlightened, unfulfilled.

Darago had always believed fate held some higher design for him. Yet here he languished, to be tossed idly into a stewpot by monsters. Where was the meaning in that?

In his youth, Darago had sought wisdom, plumbing the depths of consciousness and the soul. But no inner light penetrated the darkness surrounding him now. He was alone, afraid, abandoned even by the synchronistic forces he’d always relied upon.

With no hope left, Darago let his feelings of betrayal and anger wash over him. He had been a fool, seeing patterns and omens where there were none. The world moved blindly, without purpose or poetry. His faith now seemed the product of youthful naïveté.

The halflings outside cackled, whetting their blades with frenzied delight. Darago bowed his head, embracing the futility and emptiness of his existence. A single tear traced down his cheek. He had thought himself destined for enlightenment and transformation. Instead oblivion awaited.

ACT III

Twelve

BREAK INTO ACT 3

Sam reappears and rescues Darago from the halflings.

Darago knelt crestfallen in his cell, the halflings’ giggling and smacking lips haunting him from outside the barred door. He jumped with shock when a scraping sound came from the tiny window above. Daring not to hope, he looked up to see two wide, familiar eyes peering in.

“Sam!” Darago cried hoarsely. “You’re alive!”

“And kickin’!” Sam replied, though still clearly in pain from his injury. “Couldn’t leave you like this.”

Darago’s heart swelled. Perhaps fate had brought them together for a purpose after all.

Sam produced a key pilfered from a sleeping guard and unlocked the cell. Helping Darago up, he led them stealthily down the hall. They hid in an alcove as halflings shuffled past, avoiding detection.

Escaping the building, they dashed through twisting alleys, losing any pursuit. Darago breathed deep the free air, invigorated. Though not out of danger yet, with Sam’s aid he was reborn to continue his quest.

Sam smiled, reading Darago’s restored hope. “We stand together, come what may,” he said firmly. Darago saw that though Sam seemed ever-cheerful, an iron will dwelled beneath. The boy had conquered agony and fear to selflessly risk all for friendship. Noble spirits truly bound them.

They slipped quietly from the city like thieves in the night. The road ahead was unclear, but Darago felt galvanized. What power had brought Sam to his rescue Darago knew not, yet it whispered of secrets still untapped within them both. Destinies interwoven, they would unravel the mysteries ahead side by side.

Thirteen

FINALE

Darago succeeds in his potion quest thanks to Sam’s help.

Clearing the city walls, Darago and Sam hurried into the surrounding woods to hide. Both were weary and battered, but freedom lifted their spirits.

“We make quite the team,” Sam said with a grin. “But what now?”

Darago’s mind turned again to his quest. So close to obtaining the potion he had traveled countless leagues for. Could he risk returning to Ximes?

Sensing his thoughts, Sam spoke up eagerly. “I can sneak back in and find this potion for you! Just describe it.”

Darago pondered only a moment before nodding. If anyone could manage it, Sam could. Darago detailed the potion’s distinct azure hue and markings on the bottle.

They agreed to meet at dawn’s first light. As Sam snuck back towards Ximes, Darago found a secluded spot to wait and pray for his friend’s success.

The hours passed at a crawl. Finally, as the first rays of sun peeked through the trees, Darago heard rustling. Sam emerged triumphantly, clutching the glowing blue potion!

Darago took the bottle with trembling hands. “However did you…”

“I have my ways,” Sam said with a wink. “Told you we’d see your quest through.”

Overjoyed, Darago drank the potion he had so long pursued. Energy and purpose surged within him anew. Their trials had only made success sweeter.

Fourteen

FINAL IMAGE

Darago and Sam ride off together, odd couple friendship cemented.

A new day dawned as Darago and Sam prepared to leave Ximes far behind. Though marked by injuries and grief, their spirits were high. Shared hardship had forged an unbreakable bond between them.

Sam brought the stagecoach round, repaired again by his resourceful hands. As they boarded, Sam turned to Darago with a solemn look.

“I know you have your own path to follow, now you’ve got your potion. But we sure made a good team these past days.”

Darago smiled. “That we did. I confess, when we first met I thought you a mere overeager youth. But I see now your heart is true and courage vast.”

Sam reddened at the praise. “And I thought you a stick in the mud! But you’re the wisest, most determined man I know.”

“Perhaps…” Darago mused. “We might continue on together. True friends are rare as sapphires. If you will have me, I would be honored to remain by your side.”

Joy lit up Sam’s face. “You just try and get rid of me now!” Laughing, they clasped hands. This odd pair were now brothers-in-arms.

The stagecoach rattled down the road towards new horizons. But Darago knew their true destination was each other’s company. In saving him, Sam had filled the empty spaces in Darago’s soul. And Sam had found in Darago the wisdom and steadfastness he had lost. Two wayward souls, joined by destiny into one unshakeable friendship that would endure all storms ahead.
