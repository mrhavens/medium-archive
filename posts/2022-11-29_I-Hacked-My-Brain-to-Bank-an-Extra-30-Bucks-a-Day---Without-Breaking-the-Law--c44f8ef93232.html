I Hacked My Brain to Make an Extra 30 Bucks a Day — Without Breaking the Law!

How I increased my cash flow by harnessing the power of addiction

My brain is a complex machine.

It has its moments.

But I feel like there’s a constant tug-of-war between the part of my brain that thinks I should make progress towards some long-term goal — like writing a book — and the part of my brain that feels like it should focus on something short-term — like taking a nap.

It takes effort to create a new habit.

I often find myself struggling to stay focused and develop new thought patterns.

But I am always looking for new ways to challenge myself

I do my best to avoid any mental blocks, especially with writing.

And I’m slowly learning that progress in something can be made gradually.

Historically, I tend to double down and do things all at once.

But that’s not exactly sustainable if I have larger goals that need more than a few days, weeks, or months of my time.

Don’t Break the Chain.
It’s a productive hack popularized by comedian Jerry Seinfeld.

Don’t Break the Chain involves tracking your daily progress on a given goal over a long period of time.

By marking off each day you work on your goal, you create a chain you don’t want to break.

This motivates you to keep up your productivity and work on your goal.

When I read about this strategy for the first time, I knew it would work for me.

I knew my brain.

We got well acquainted sometime in my 30s.

And we both knew that this brain hack would work for us.

So, I went to a random website and printed out a calendar for the following month like this one (not an affiliate link).

I pulled out a black sharpie, and at the top of the calendar, I wrote the following in big, bold, black letters:

MAKE AN EXTRA 30 DOLLARS A DAY BY ANY MEANS NECESSARY

This was a rather impulsive thing to commit to. I just signed a contract with my brain to spend the following month doing God knows what.

At the time, there wasn’t an urgent need for an extra 30 dollars a day.

Well, that wasn’t exactly true. There was a need. I was in the throes of divorce and needed money to pay legal fees. I suppose I didn’t feel the urgency I should have felt in those months; that whole process was a pain my brain was trying to avoid. Perhaps it didn’t associate that need with this particular adventure at the time, but I shall leave that for another story.

The goal wasn’t to make money because of pending financial doom. The goal was to hack my brain to create the same sense of urgency I might feel when faced with pending financial doom.

I needed to feel compelled to make money without a directly correlated financial motivation. Also, I restricted myself to only pursue new avenues of making money that I had not explored. After all, I was mostly in it for the growth, novelty, and dopamine response.

The following day was the first of the month, so I had at least one day to do some research.

The level of excitement was pretty exhilarating.

I was already feeling the pressure.

The empty calendar lying next to my keyboard was going to need a big, fat, black X marked through the first of the month by the end of the following day.

Only a few moments had passed, and I had already hacked my sense of guilt with a one-cent sheet of printer paper.

I pulled out some blank notecards from an old shoebox that had been untouched for nearly a decade.

I needed to brainstorm.

There were no options that I considered off the table at this point. I needed ideas, and I needed them fast.

I wrote out one idea on each of the cards.

Blood plasma
Secret Shopper
Food Deliveries
Grocery Deliveries
Ridesharing
Paid Dungeon Master
Prostitution
Upwork
Fiverr

I was starting without a plan.

I had no direction. No outline.

I was making this up as I went along.

All I knew is that I needed to make 30 dollars by the end of the following day.

Prostitution?

I tossed it out. I live in Texas, not Nevada. It wasn’t a serious consideration. I just wrote it down as it foolishly came into my mind.

Although I may have momentarily rationalized that I could use the experience as field research for a future writing project.

I tore the card into many pieces and discarded it.

I spent a few hours downloading, installing, and registering apps. By the time I registered the third app, I was already on autopilot, mindlessly plugging away my contact information and uploading images of my driver’s license and my tags and insurance information.

There were glitches, background checks, and waits for nearly all of them. But I got the process started on all that I could find.

Upwork and Fiverr were already established from prior freelance work. However, I knew it would still take time to get into that groove and find opportunities, especially in new and unexplored work areas.

By this point, I needed 30 dollars within 24 hours. I needed something certain to pay off by then. I knew I would need more time than that to find a paying client willing to take on a newbie. So, I set those two cards aside.

I found a handful of secret shopper apps that looks promising. But I wasn’t sure if it could deliver 30 dollars by the next day. I set that card aside.

I knew I could make the paid dungeon master thing work, and for the most part, I did — but not that week. I’ll save that adventure for another story.

All that was left was blood plasma.

It was almost midnight by that point, so after about 20 minutes of research, I decided that the next day, I would pay a visit to the closest blood plasma place near me.

On their website, they guaranteed 1000 dollars if I could donate 8 times in a month.

I never donated blood plasma before, so I wasn’t sure what to expect.

But when I arrived the next day, I felt like a made a fool out of myself, because there was no clear signage for new donors and the staff all seemed to be on autopilot. They didn’t recognize that I was a new doner.

Too proud to ask for help while simultaneously feeling guilty that I might bother such busy people, I tried to figure out what to do by watching what everyone else was doing.

Everyone else was already a doner, so they were doing their thing and getting paid already.

I, on the other hand, needed to undergo the initial two-hour-long intake procedure.

There were interviews, videos, and a bunch of questions I had to answer on a touch screen. They even recorded my reading speed.

I discovered it was embarrassingly slow.

Maybe I’m a bit judgy when it comes to a company’s customer-facing technology, but when I see a 20-year-old computer running on a 4:3 ratio monitor, I begin to have mixed feelings about the company’s priorities.

Once the intake was finished, they hooked me up and we conducted the transaction. In exchange for 100 milliliters of high-quality blood plasma, they issued 125 dollars to my new debit card.

I spent an hour on the intake process, and another hour getting drained of blood plasma.

Most of the time, I was browsing the internet on my smartphone waiting for things to finish.

Not bad for a couple of hours of work.

When I got home, I realized that I had made more than my 30-dollar goal.

125 dollars was 95 dollars in excess of what I needed for the day.

I decided, rather impulsively, that my real goal was to learn how to push myself to get creative while developing a habit.

I wasn’t going to spend the next three days sitting on my laurels.

I quickly made the decision that it didn’t matter what I made that day.

It didn’t matter if was 30 dollars, 300 dollars, or 3000 dollars.

What mattered was that I made at least 30 dollars every single day so that I could use my big, fat, black sharpie and cross off the next number on the calendar.

I went to my calendar and read again the big, bold letters that I had written the day before.

MAKE AN EXTRA 30 DOLLARS A DAY BY ANY MEANS NECESSARY

There was no escaping this contract.

I had to make an extra 30 dollars a day, by any means necessary.

It didn’t matter that I made 125 dollars.

I had to start all over again the next day.

And that’s exactly what I did.

I pulled out my big, fat, black sharpie and marked a satisfying X on the 1st box — the first of the month.

It was done.

1 down, 30 more to go.

Days 2, 3, and 4 each progressed with various levels of excitement.

And I experimented with every money-making app I could find.

After a while, I figured out how to optimize my time so that I could make 30 dollars in a day as quickly as possible.

All of those specifics, I may save for another story.

In any case, this strategy seemed to work well for me.

It worked so well for me, in fact, that I’m afraid to commit to it again.

By the end of the first month, I had filled the calendar with an unbroken string of thirty-one Xs.

I became addicted.

I became so addicted that by the time I marked off my last X, I printed a new calendar and started it all over again for a second month.

And then a third.

So, if you’re considering starting a new habit, I strongly recommend Don’t Break the Chain.
