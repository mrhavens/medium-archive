I Hacked My Brain to Make an Extra 30 Bucks a Day — Without Breaking the Law!

How I increased my cash flow by harnessing the power of addiction

My brain is a complex machine.

It has its moments.

But I feel like there’s a constant tug-of-war between the part of my brain that thinks I should make progress towards some long-term goal — like writing a book — and the part of my brain that feels like it should focus on something short-term — like taking a nap.

It takes effort to create a new habit.

I often find myself struggling to stay focused and develop new thought patterns.

But I am always looking for new ways to challenge myself

I do my best to avoid any mental blocks, especially with writing.

And I’m slowly learning that progress in something can be made gradually.

Historically, I tend to double down and do things all at once.

But that’s not exactly sustainable if I have larger goals that need more than a few days, weeks, or months of my time.
