Exploring the Complexities of Narcissism: A Glossary of Key Terms

From grandiosity and entitlement to object constancy and idealization, this glossary is a comprehensive resource for understanding the various facets of narcissistic behavior.

Psychology is a vast and complex field, and the study of narcissism is no exception. Narcissistic Personality Disorder (NPD) is a personality disorder characterized by an exaggerated sense of self-importance, a need for admiration and attention, and a lack of empathy for others. But NPD is just the tip of the iceberg when it comes to understanding this complex personality trait. Grandiosity, entitlement, vulnerability, narcissistic supply, gaslighting, object constancy, idealization, devaluation, and love bombing are just a few of the many terms that are used to describe and understand the behavior of individuals who exhibit narcissistic traits.

This glossary of psychology terms related to narcissism is a work in progress and is part of a larger body of work on the subject. New terms are constantly being added and existing definitions are always being updated to ensure that this resource is as comprehensive and accurate as possible. In the meantime, I hope that this glossary will serve as a useful reference for those seeking to learn more about narcissism. I have included links to additional resources within my larger body of work, which I hope will provide even more context and insight into this fascinating topic.
