Hoovering: When Narcissists Try to Suck You Back In

From social media to showing up unannounced, understanding and surviving this manipulative tactic

Have you ever had someone from your past, who you suspect may have narcissistic tendencies, try to re-establish contact with you after a period of separation or estrangement? This tactic, known as hoovering, is a common tool used by narcissists in order to manipulate and draw their former partners or victims back into their orbit. In this article, we’ll explore the definition and consequences of hoovering, and provide tips on how to recognize and protect yourself from this behavior when it is used by individuals with narcissistic traits.

Hoovering is a tactic often used by individuals with narcissistic tendencies in order to draw a former partner or victim back into their orbit after a period of separation or estrangement. The narcissist may use various methods to try to re-establish contact, such as reaching out through social media, sending texts or emails, or even showing up unannounced.

The goal of hoovering is often to manipulate the target back into the relationship or to re-establish the narcissist’s supply of attention, admiration, or other forms of validation. It can be especially confusing and difficult to deal with because the narcissist may appear to be remorseful or understanding, or may try to make the target feel guilty for leaving.

It is important to be aware of hoovering and to have a plan in place for dealing with it. This may include setting boundaries, such as blocking the narcissist’s contact information or enlisting the help of trusted friends or family members. It may also be helpful to seek the advice of a therapist or support group. Remember, you have the right to protect yourself and your own well-being.
