Building in Public: The Writer’s Guide to Boosting Productivity, Audience Reach, and Impact

A proven strategy for writers to overcome obstacles, market their work, and get feedback to improve their writing skills

Are you struggling with writer’s block or procrastination as a writer? Are you looking for ways to market your work and get feedback to improve it? Building in Public may be the solution you are looking for.

Building in Public is a strategy that originated in the startup world, but it can be applied to the world of writing. It involves sharing your writing and the behind-the-scenes of your creative process with an audience. This can be done through social media, blogging, podcasting, or starting a YouTube channel. By building in public, you will be able to increase your productivity, reach a larger audience, and get valuable feedback to improve your work.

Increase Productivity

Building in Public can help you overcome writer’s block and procrastination. When you know that your work will be shared with an audience, you become more motivated and focused to write. Additionally, feedback from your audience can help identify areas that need improvement, leading to an increase in productivity.

Reach a Larger Audience

Sharing your work on social media or a website can help you reach a wider audience than you would have been able to otherwise. Furthermore, building your email list enables you to connect with your readers and promote your work.

Get Valuable Feedback

The most important benefit of Building in Public is that it can help you create more impactful and meaningful work. Feedback from your audience can help you identify what works well and what does not, as well as provide new ideas for your writing.

Building in Public is a strategy that can help writers overcome obstacles, market their work, and improve their writing skills. By sharing the creative process and finished works with an audience, you will be able to increase productivity, reach a larger audience, and get valuable feedback. While it may seem daunting at first, the benefits are well worth it.
