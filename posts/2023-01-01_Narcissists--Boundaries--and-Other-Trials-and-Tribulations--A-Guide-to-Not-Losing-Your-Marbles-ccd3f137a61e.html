Narcissists, Boundaries, and Other Trials and Tribulations: A Guide to Not Losing Your Marbles

Published on January 01, 2023 at 05:25 PM

NEUTRALIZING NARCISSISM

Narcissists, Boundaries, and Other Trials and Tribulations: A Guide to Not Losing Your Marbles

My insights on maintaining your sanity — or at least pretending like you have some

As someone who has had the pleasure of dealing with a covert narcissist on a regular basis, let me tell you, boundaries are crucial.

Now, I’m not saying I’m some kind of boundary-setting guru or anything like that. In fact, there have been times when I’ve been about as effective at setting boundaries as a screen door on a submarine. But despite my own shortcomings, I’ve learned that boundaries are essential for maintaining a healthy dynamic in any relationship.

Especially unavoidably toxic, dysfunctional ones.

So, what are boundaries, you might ask?

Well, they’re essentially the lines that we draw around ourselves to define what is and is not acceptable behavior from others. They help to establish a sense of personal autonomy and respect and are essential for keeping things running smoothly in any relationship.

Without clear boundaries, it’s easy for people to become overly enmeshed with one another, leading to conflicts and misunderstandings.

Sometimes these boundaries might require a court order. God help you if this is the case.

If you’re that far along in your battle with a narcissist, chances are they’ve already made you look like the bad guy. It’s very important for their own sense of self-worth that they do this because they depend so much on external validation. They have no inner strength. They know that deep down, they’re just a piece of shit. They just want to manipulate the world into telling them that they’re not.

It’s a really messed up world they live in. I wouldn’t recommend joining them.

I know what you’re thinking.

“But Mark, how do I set boundaries with someone who has no regard for the feelings or needs of others? Won’t they just steamroll right over me and do whatever they want?”

If it’s really that bad, get a court order.

I’m only half-serious.

I voiced only the idea of a court order to my narcissist once, and I didn’t see them again for 6 months. They came hoovering back after I forgot all about their bullshit.

It’s certainly a challenge.

But it’s not an impossible challenge.

By being clear and consistent in communicating our boundaries, and by enforcing them when necessary, we can help to establish a more healthy and functional dynamic in our interactions with these… people.

Now, I’m not saying that setting boundaries is always easy, or that it will solve all of our problems. But by being aware of our own boundaries and communicating them effectively to… those around us, we can go a long way towards creating more healthy and fulfilling relationships.

So, the next time you find yourself dealing with a covert narcissist (or any other challenging personality, for that matter), remember to keep your boundaries in mind.

Don’t be afraid to enforce boundaries when necessary.

And don’t be delusional about it. That’s the narcissist’s job.

It might take some boldness.

It might be an uncomfortable conversation.

Maybe the relationship as you know it will end.

It might even lead to a court battle that will make your life a living hell.

But, it might also save you a lot of headaches and pain in the long run.

And again, this is especially important when dealing with someone who has a tendency to be manipulative or disrespectful of others’ needs.

Like narcissists.

My narcissist had lots of ways of telling me that I disrespected their need for me to be their meat puppet.

I was literally told that I needed to murder my sense of self, and yield to them unconditionally.

They even quoted the Bible, many, many times. It was their thing. They were really good at it.

It’s bullshit.

When you set boundaries, they will retaliate and act like a child.

They might act like a grown-up on the surface, but they will have the stunted emotional intelligence of a teenager for the rest of their lives.

Their emotional development stopped at puberty.

They will think of you as a Judas.

They will believe you betrayed them.

And that’s perfectly fine.

Because that’s on them, not you.

You have the right to respect and dignity.

Taking away their power to violate you as a person isn’t something that they will ever want for themselves.

You are their source of internal validation.

They have nothing internally that can provide validation for them. They only have you. And to them, you’re broken.

To hell with them.

But, it’s important to remember that it’s okay to say “fuck off” in the most respectful way possible, and to set limits on how we allow others to treat us.

It’s okay to literally say “fuck off” also. But do so with discernment and care. These people might spend the rest of their lives getting revenge on even the smallest of slights.

I just want to make sure people know that we don’t have to put up with shit from anyone, and it’s okay to cut people out of our lives if they consistently fail to respect our boundaries.

This may or may not be possible — cutting toxic people out of our lives. It might be something we have to put up with forever.

That’s okay too.

They’re going to think that you setting these boundaries for them as an encroachment on their entitlement and as a slap in their face.

They’re going to think that you’re violating THEIR boundaries.

From their perspective, being a total piece of shit is just part of who they are as a person

And deep down, they know it

They know it, or they wouldn’t try so hard to make other people see them for who they desperately want to be.

Who they REALLY are isn’t at all who they want to be.

And if YOU can’t see THEM for the delicious piece of sweet chocolate cake that they carefully created just for you — that you selfishly are unwilling to allow to be shoved down your throat in order to make them feel good themselves — then, by their account YOU’RE the toxic piece of shit, not them.

They really think this about you.

See how toxic this is? It’s not healthy.

There’s no redemption for the narcissist.

There’s no therapy for them that can make them healthy.

They’re too far gone.

But that doesn’t mean setting boundaries is impossible.

It just means that it will be an ongoing, lifelong war.

An unwinnable war in which setting healthy boundaries is only half of every battle.

The other half is enforcing them.

But it’s important to spend this lifelong war standing firm and not backing down.

Forever.

It can be helpful to have a support system in place.

Not all family and friends are able to deal with your lifelong war of ongoing, unending, narcissistically induced trauma. Lean on them with discernment, love, and care for their well-being.

Therapists are probably the best bet for most people. Let’s pay them what they’re worth so that we can stay reasonably sane and hold our boundaries.

In the end, setting and enforcing boundaries is all about taking care of ourselves.

It’s about respecting ourselves.

It’s about recognizing our own worth and not allowing others to treat us in ways that are harmful or disrespectful.

Without the delusions and manipulation that separate us from them.

And with acknowledgment and accountability for our own brokenness.

And compassion. For us, and for them.

They’re broken, and they always will be.

That’s pitiable.

We’re broken, but we can change, adapt, and heal.

That’s admirable.

So, the next time you find yourself dealing with a covert narcissist (or anyone else who tries to push your buttons), remember to set your boundaries and don’t be afraid to enforce them.

It might just save you from dealing with even more bullshit in the long run.
