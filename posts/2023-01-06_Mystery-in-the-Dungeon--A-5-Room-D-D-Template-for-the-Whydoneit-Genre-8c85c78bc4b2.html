Mystery in the Dungeon: A 5-Room D&D Template for the Whydoneit Genre

A 5 room dungeon is a type of adventure design that consists of five main structural elements, or “rooms,” that represent different parts of the story or challenges that the players must face. The rooms are typically connected in a linear fashion, with the players moving from one room to the next until they reach the end of the dungeon. The concept of a 5 room dungeon is inspired by the idea that a good story should have a clear beginning, middle, and end, and that these structural elements can be divided into smaller sections or “beats” for the purpose of pacing and storytelling. In a 5 room dungeon, the first room is typically the introduction or setup, the second and third rooms are the rising action or challenges, the fourth room is the climax or major turning point, and the fifth room is the resolution or conclusion. This structure can be adapted and modified to fit different types of stories and genres and is often used in tabletop role-playing games such as Dungeons & Dragons (D&D).

“Save the Cat” is a screenwriting methodology developed by Blake Snyder. He suggests that a story should include a “Save the Cat” moment, in which the main character does something likable and relatable early on in the story to endear themselves to the audience.

There are several benefits to combining a 5 room dungeon with the “Save the Cat” Whydoneit genre:

  1. It provides a clear structure: Using the 5 room dungeon structure allows you to create a clear and logical progression for your adventure, with each room serving a specific purpose. This can help keep the story focused and prevent it from becoming too scattered or disorganized.
  2. It adds mystery and suspense: The Whydoneit genre is all about mystery and suspense, and using a 5 room dungeon allows you to build on these elements as the players progress through the adventure. Each room can reveal new clues and twists, keeping the players engaged and guessing until the final confrontation.
  3. It encourages problem-solving: The Whydoneit genre requires the players to gather and interpret clues in order to solve the mystery. Using a 5 room dungeon allows you to present these clues in a structured and logical way, encouraging the players to think critically and work together to solve the puzzle.
  4. It allows for customization: The 5 room dungeon structure is flexible and can be customized to fit your specific adventure. You can add or remove rooms as needed, and tailor the challenges and clues to fit your story. This allows you to create a unique and memorable experience for your players.

Overall, combining a 5 room dungeon with the Whydoneit genre allows you to create a structured, suspenseful, and engaging mystery adventure for your tabletop role-playing group.
