Understanding Pathological Lying in Narcissism

The connection between narcissistic personality disorder and compulsive lying

Are you or a loved one struggling with compulsive lying? It may be a symptom of Narcissistic Personality Disorder. Learn about the relationship between NPD and pathological lying and how to seek treatment.

Pathological lying, a type of compulsive or excessive lying not caused by external factors such as financial gain or coercion, can be a symptom of Narcissistic Personality Disorder (NPD). This type of lying can cause significant challenges in interpersonal relationships, and understanding the link between NPD and pathological lying can aid in seeking treatment.

Narcissistic Personality Disorder is characterized by a need for admiration, fantasies of immense importance, and a lack of empathy for others. These traits may lead to pathological lying as a person with NPD may want to maintain a false image of themselves or achieve admiration and special treatment from others. It’s not clear if a person with a history of pathological lying is aware of the deceit or able to think rationally about it. Although there is little research on the causes of pathological lying, some studies show that pathological lying is a common feature in several conditions, such as personality disorders, and factitious disorders.

Pathological lying can be a symptom of Narcissistic Personality Disorder (NPD) which can cause significant challenges in interpersonal relationships. Understanding the link between NPD and pathological lying can aid in seeking treatment. It’s important to note that pathological lying is not exclusive to NPD, it can be a symptom of other personality disorders or conditions, and other factors such as environmental or developmental may play a role. If you suspect that you or a loved one may be struggling with NPD, it is essential to seek professional help from a therapist or psychologist. They can help understand and manage symptoms, and work towards overall well-being and improved relationships.
