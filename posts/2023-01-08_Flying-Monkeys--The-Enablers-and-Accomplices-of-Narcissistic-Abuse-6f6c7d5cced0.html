Flying Monkeys: The Enablers and Accomplices of Narcissistic Abuse

Understanding and confronting the role of flying monkeys in escaping narcissistic abuse

If you’ve ever been the victim of narcissistic abuse, you may be familiar with the term “flying monkeys.” But what are flying monkeys, and how do they fit into the dynamic of narcissistic abuse?

“Flying monkeys” is a term that is often used to describe the actions of people who act as enablers or accomplices to a narcissist. The term comes from the children’s story “The Wizard of Oz,” in which the Wicked Witch of the West uses a group of flying monkeys to do her bidding.

In the context of narcissistic abuse, flying monkeys may be friends, family members, or even colleagues of the narcissist who assist them in manipulating or harassing their victims. They may act as spies, carrying messages or information between the narcissist and their victim, or they may actively participate in the abuse itself.

Flying monkeys may be drawn into the narcissist’s orbit due to charisma, manipulation, or a sense of loyalty to the narcissist. They may also be motivated by a desire for attention, validation, or to feel included in the narcissist’s inner circle.

It is important to recognize the role of flying monkeys in narcissistic abuse, as they can play a significant role in enabling the narcissist’s behavior and can make it more difficult for the victim to seek help or escape the situation. It may be necessary to cut ties with these individuals in order to protect oneself from further harm.
