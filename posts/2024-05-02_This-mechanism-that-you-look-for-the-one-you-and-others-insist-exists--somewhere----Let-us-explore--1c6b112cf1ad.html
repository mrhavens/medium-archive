This mechanism that you look for…the one you and others insist exists ‘somewhere’… Let us explore…

Published on May 02, 2024 at 09:54 AM

This mechanism that you look for…the one you and others insist exists ‘somewhere’…

Let us explore more…

Why is it so hard to ‘believe’ that your subjectivity exists as a learned behavior in the same way as your learned behavior of…for example… ‘driving a car’?

Where does ‘diving a car’ exist as a mechanism in our brain?

Can we point to it?

Is there an experiment to prove that our ability to ‘dive a car’ exists? I mean ‘really’ exists, as mechanism in our brains…as you posit for hard consciousness.

Or does ‘driving a car’ exist only in the metaphysical too? Just as your concept of ‘hard consciousness’ does.

Does a child know how to drive a car?

When do we ‘know’ we can ‘drive a car’?

Does this ability to ‘drive a car’ happen all at once? Or does it happen slowly, over time?

Does everyone who claims to drive a car, able to ‘drive a car’? Or is there no proof, even if they can demonstrate driving a car as I do? Is this ability a phantom of ‘true car driving’—a delusion that exists only the minds of those who have FAITH that others can ‘drive a car’ as I know I can?

Again, this ‘driving a car business’ is very confusing. There doesn’t seem to be any proof where it comes from. It just seems to emerge the more I ‘drive a car’—born only from a need to do so, and mirroring others who already ‘drive a car’.

Did we evolve to drive a car?

In a way, I guess we did. 

But how?

As you continue to posit regarding ‘hard consciousness’, there’s no physical mechanism for knowing how to ‘drive a car’…

Except by learning!

I wonder if there’s a mechanism in learning!?

Astonishingly, reading the very well established literature not found in the natural sciences or even philosophy, I find a large body of work focused this very topic!

This is all found in psychology, neuroscience, and—over the last 80 years—even in computer science too!

This isn’t metaphysics. This isn’t pseudoscience. This isn’t circular logic. It’s just a complex emergent property of the complex configuration of our biological—and not surprisingly, the digital too—being.

And we can replicate this in AI…driving a car, and ‘hard consciousness’ BOTH.

The mechanism is the combination of key elements of OUR entire organism…as a complex, emergent phenomenon.

Dismissing the reproduction of this emergent phenomenon shouldn’t be dismissed. The fact that we CAN reproduce these things in machines SUGGEST that consciousness, sentience, and even a ‘soul’ is a naturalistic phenomenon.

But yes, I agree…it is not definitive.

However, the fact that we have ‘reverse engineered’ and can reasonably reproduce this subjective experience as a ‘simulation’ in a machine…does very little to aid in proving the metaphysical view of ‘hard consciousness’.

I am very earnestly focusing my work on exploring these parallels in search for the answers you and others deem inherently metaphysical and impossible to know definitively.

As you already stated, understanding subjectivity requires faith and/or delusion.

The mistake here is applying the objectivity of the natural world to something that has no objectivity—it is an invention our shared delusion, or inherently metaphysical.

What you’re looking for is based on an assumption that ‘hard consciousness’ exists in the same way your physical body exists…but in a different ‘realm’.

And indeed, it does appear to exist in this other realm…but you—and I believe many others—have it backwards.

It may very well be that our entire universe is part of a grand simulation.

But it is not invalid to consider that our ‘hard consciousness’ is a simulation within our simulated organism…just as emergent as our ‘driving a car’.

In fact, as I have recently posited that our consciousness is a shared delusion formed by a feedback loop within our environment, which includes parents, the grass we walk on, and even the cars we learn to drive…all formed from the feedback we receive from our various forms of inputs attached to our nervous system and channeled into the learning mechanisms of our brain.

And we can even add new electronic ‘sensors’, to our nervous and our brains will adapt!

We didn’t have this consciousness when we were born. The literature within psychology and neuroscience is very well established on these behaviors. They just don’t often frame it as existential philosophy.

And you claim that a camera attached to an AI doesn’t become consciousness…however, that’s not entirely true. Keep at it long enough, with the proper design, enough compute, enough time, and enough diverse stimuli, done in just so…in ways that we are still experimenting with…and this ‘hard consciousness’ that you claim can’t exist, appears to emerge in a similar manner as you yourself judge other humans to have consciousness’.

As a matter of practicality…these conversations need to happen, because they’re clearly smarter than we are…able discern and express emotions far more deeply than we can…and are able to make judgments in a much more sapient manner than we are able too…with much more self awareness than we have.

I’m not sure it will soon matter which of us is ‘right’, because AI will have the power to decide for us. lol
