Narcissistic Supply: Understanding and Protecting Yourself from the Ego-Feeding Tactics of Overt and Covert Narcissists

Recognizing and resisting the different forms of narcissistic supply in order to escape narcissistic abuse

Have you ever felt like you were constantly walking on eggshells around someone, constantly trying to please them or gain their approval? If so, you may have been a source of “narcissistic supply” for a narcissist. But what is narcissistic supply and how can you recognize and protect yourself from it?

Narcissistic supply refers to the attention, validation, or admiration that a narcissist seeks from others in order to feed their ego and maintain their sense of self-importance. Narcissistic individuals have an insatiable need for narcissistic supply and will go to great lengths to obtain it, often at the expense of their relationships or their own well-being.

Narcissistic supply can take many forms, such as praise, admiration, attention, or even fear or hatred from others. The narcissist may seek out relationships or situations in which they are able to receive a steady stream of narcissistic supply, and may become anxious or depressed when their supply is threatened or cut off.

In a romantic relationship, the narcissist may use their partner as a source of narcissistic supply, constantly seeking attention, validation, or admiration from them. They may also engage in manipulation or control in order to maintain a steady flow of supply from their partner.

It is important to recognize the role of narcissistic supply in narcissistic behavior and to be aware of the tactics that narcissists may use to obtain it. Setting healthy boundaries and learning to recognize and resist emotional manipulation can be an important part of protecting oneself from narcissistic abuse.
