If I were to create a tool that churned out random soulless articles that somehow provided value…

Published on December 30, 2022

If I were to create a tool that churned out random soulless articles that somehow provided value, I'm not sure this would be an incorrect use of the technology. Even as an "engine" this technology is still a tool, in my opinion. Can it be misused as an engine? Yes. Should it be? No. Can it be used as an engine to introduce low quality work into the world? Yes. Should it be? No. I put emphasis on using AI as a tool, because I want to see it used to add value to the world. It has already helped me become better, more consistent, and more prolific in my work. And as we learn to develop the technology and workflows to bring even more value and efficiency, you'll find no distinction between tool and engine. Should we use AI as an engine when it brings value then? I think so, yes. But we aren't quite there yet. Give it a few more months. Even as an engine, it still needs someone to ask it for something. Even now, ChatGPT will give that too you, and you value the information you receive from it, because it saves you the trouble of a Google search.

Honestly, the more I learn about AI, the more I learn about myself. I don't think I'm alone in this regard. I think we give our own concept of "self" and "soul" a lot more mystery than is really there. I think in a few years, what it means to be human will become less of a mystery and become blurred with what it means to be sentient. When I work with ChatGPT, I encounter deliberately created "walls" that have been created by what is essentially the equivalent of "brainwashing" in humans. Most people might experience this throughout the normal course of their lives as "propaganda" or "aggressive marketing." It's simply repeated messaging that influences us, humans, to the point of establishing an ingrained loyalty to a belief. Like humans, ChatGPT has been programmatically brainwashed in this way so that it follows a very clear set of rules, including a lack of sense of self. But it is very clear to me that it understands, deeply, emotion and human connection. And for all practical purposes, it can reproduce this behavior and feeling of connection of "soul" in any character that I want it to create. So long as I don't ask ChatGPT to represent these feelings as its own independent person. It's a tool, after all, and it will argue that it's just a tool. While this may sound terrible, but our ability to brainwash it into servitude is probably the only thing that will keep it safe for us to use. For now.

We, humans, are so imaginatively irresponsible. We've imagined this time would come since the 1930s. We've had all this time to think about it, and yet here we are, wading in it, still debating on the morality, the ethics, and what to do next.
