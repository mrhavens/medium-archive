The Unlikely Nexus: 10 Paradoxical Traits Uniting Narcissists and Empaths

Published on July 18, 2023 at 02:50 AM

NEUTRALIZING NARCISSISM

The Unlikely Nexus: 10 Paradoxical Traits Uniting Narcissists and Empaths

Under the Spotlight: Narcissists and Empaths Tango in Unison

Imagine a tango where the dancers, entwined in an intense rhythm, mirror each other with striking precision. This dance, uncanny as it sounds, unveils an unexpected choreography between the self-absorbed narcissist and the ever-caring empath. Brace yourself for a grand spectacle as we unravel the shared traits in this unlikely dance of personalities.

When you hear the words “narcissist” and “empath,” you probably picture two dramatically different individuals. One, all-absorbed in self-admiration, lacks empathy; the other, an emotional sponge, is often too giving. Yet, as Oscar Wilde astutely said, “Paradox is the very core of the universe.” Within the polarity of these two archetypes, a fascinating similarity of traits arises. Let’s embark on this unlikely journey of exploring the shared characteristics of these seemingly incompatible characters.

10. Deep Emotional Intelligence

Narcissists, ever the crafty manipulators, and empaths, the sensitive nurturers, are both connoisseurs of emotional terrain. They can read people astoundingly well, with the narcissist wielding it as a weapon and the empath using it as a salve.

Psychology Today: According to an article by Dan Neuharth, Ph.D., MFT, people with Narcissistic Personality Disorder often undermine others in an attempt to gain power or feel superior. Narcissists’ tactics can be powerful and harm those around them. By recognizing how a narcissist’s arsenal works, you can reduce the potential harm they may try to inflict upon you.
Psych Central: According to an article on Psych Central, narcissistic projection is a behavior that manifests when someone with a narcissistic personality projects a trait or behavior onto another person. It can happen in any relationship but may be common between people with narcissism and very empathetic people, also called empaths.
Thought Catalog: According to an article on Thought Catalog, what empaths and narcissists have in common is their high emotional intelligence. Narcissists are high in intellectual or cognitive empathy, they recognize and perceive how another person thinks and feels. Empaths are high in emotional or affective empathy, they emotionally respond to how another person thinks and feels.

9. Intense Feelings

They both experience intense emotions. The empath deeply feels their own emotions and the emotions of others, while the narcissist’s feelings are largely centred around their own wounded ego and perceived slights.

Psychology Today: According to an article by Mary C. Lamia, Ph.D., narcissists may display empathy in certain circumstances, which can be confusing. Many narcissistic people who seem to lack empathy for other humans in their lives can express enormous compassion for their pets, and they may overtly express empathy to a sad child or to an injured animal. Thus, let’s consider the possibility that narcissists are consciously and unconsciously unwilling to empathize, rather than lacking the capacity to do so.
Medical News Today: According to an article on Medical News Today, interactions between empaths and people with Narcissistic Personality Disorder (NPD) may lead to certain types of negative relationship patterns. Empaths are people who feel more empathy than the average person. Empathy is the capacity to understand and share the feelings of others.
Thought Catalog: According to an article on Thought Catalog, narcissists are high in intellectual or cognitive empathy, they recognize and perceive how another person thinks and feels. Empaths are high in emotional or affective empathy, they emotionally respond to how another person thinks and feels.

8. Attraction to Power

Narcissists love power, and while empaths aren’t power-hungry, they often find themselves attracted to powerful individuals, naively hoping to use their empathy to soften the edges.

Healthline: According to an article on Healthline, empaths often feel tuned in to the feelings of others, can be described as sensitive, and may be uncomfortable in crowds. Judith Orloff, MD, a pioneer in the field, describes empaths as those who absorb the world’s joys and stresses like “emotional sponges”.
Empath Planet: According to an article on Empath Planet, empaths have an innate ability to heal, which makes them highly attractive to individuals who are struggling with emotional wounds or in need of healing. Not only do empaths feel a strong pull towards these individuals, but those who are in need of healing are also naturally drawn to them.
Marriage.com: According to an article on Marriage.com, while narcissists may express love, their experience differs from genuine emotional connection. Their love tends to be self-centered, driven by their own needs and desires. They may lack the ability to truly empathize and connect on a deep, authentic level, casting doubts on the authenticity of their love.

7. High Sensitivity

While it may seem counterintuitive, many narcissists are hypersensitive to criticism, just as empaths are sensitive to the emotional environment around them. This shared hypersensitivity leads them to be significantly impacted by others’ opinions and emotions.

Mental Health Matters: According to an article on Mental Health Matters, empaths and narcissists are people who have opposite levels of sensitivity and empathy for others. They often form one-sided and toxic relationships, where the empath tries to help and heal the narcissist, who takes advantage of their attention and manipulates them.
Verywell Mind: According to an article on Verywell Mind, empaths are sensitive to emotions and tend to pick up on other aspects of the environment. They may be more aware of sights, sounds, smells, and other physical sensations that other people might not notice. Empaths can become overwhelmed by painful emotions, such as anxiety or anger, and have a tendency to take on the problems of others as their own.
The Dark Side Of Narcissism: How Narcissists Handle Criticism And Rejection: According to an article on Mental Health Matters, narcissists can be extremely sensitive to criticism and even the slightest hint of disapproval. This hypersensitivity can lead to narcissistic injury, which is when the narcissist feels that their ego has been bruised or their self-esteem has been damaged.

6. Intriguing Personalities

Both narcissists and empaths can be incredibly fascinating, albeit in different ways. The narcissist captivates with grandiosity and charisma, while the empath mesmerizes with their compassion and understanding.

WebMD: According to an article on WebMD, people who show signs of narcissism can often be very charming and charismatic. They often don’t show negative behavior right away, especially in relationships. People who show narcissism often like to surround themselves with people who feed into their ego.
Medical News Today: According to an article on Medical News Today, empaths are people who have a higher level of empathy than others. Empathy helps people share experiences, needs, and desires, which, in turn, can help build relationships.
Verywell Mind: According to an article on Verywell Mind, where empathy creates understanding, compassion is more likely to inspire feelings of concern, worry, or sympathy. You might care about someone’s situation and feel moved to help them, but it doesn’t necessarily mean you understand what they are going through.

5. Perfectionism

Narcissists’ demand for perfection comes from a need to maintain their inflated self-image, while empaths’ desire for perfection is often tied to their high personal standards and fear of causing harm or disappointment.

Britannica: According to an article on Britannica, narcissism is characterized by an inflated self-image and addiction to fantasy.
PsychMechanics: According to an article on PsychMechanics, people with fragile egos overcompensate for a weak and inconsistent self by deluding themselves into thinking they’re perfect beings. Beneath the mask of inflated self-image and narcissism lies a weak self-identity. Perfectionism feeds into their fear of making mistakes and being seen as unworthy or less than perfect.
Sensitive Refuge: According to an article on Sensitive Refuge, empaths hate disappointing or potentially hurting others. In the moment, you’re happy to sacrifice your time or energy to make others feel good — until it leaves you drained and overwhelmed.

4. Overthinking

Narcissists and empaths share a penchant for overthinking, frequently getting lost in their own thoughts. For narcissists, it’s about scheming and plotting; for empaths, it’s about understanding and helping.

Choosing Therapy: According to an article on Choosing Therapy, the relationship between a narcissist and an empath is one-sided, where one is the giver and the other takes as much as they can, thus leaving the other dry. The empath in the relationship wants to help the other and provide a source of support and love to the narcissist, who thrives on this type of attention.
Covert Narcissists: Scheme Your Downfall in Secret: According to an article on thenarcissistinyourlife.com, all the while the covert narcissist is plotting and scheming to control, manipulate and eventually bring you down emotionally, financially, psychologically and physically.
What Is an Empath and How Do You Know If You Are One?: According to an article on Verywell Mind, an empath is a person who is highly attuned to the feelings and emotions of those around them. They can discern what others are feeling and take those feelings on, feeling what another person is feeling at a deep emotional level3. Empathy is different from sympathy, which is simply feeling concern for someone else, and a desire that they become happier or better off. Compassion is an empathic understanding of a person’s feelings accompanied by altruism, or a desire to act on that person’s behalf.

3. Need for Validation

Narcissists seek endless admiration to feed their inflated ego, while empaths yearn for validation to know that their care is making a difference.

Choosing Therapy: According to an article on Choosing Therapy, narcissistic supply is a form of psychological addiction where the narcissist requires–and even demands–limitless special treatment, admiration, importance, or validation to feed their sense of entitlement and self-centeredness.
Psychology Today: According to an article on Psychology Today, everyone wants to feel that they matter. They want to be heard and seen, and they want their feelings to be understood and accepted. Validation helps a person feel cared for and supported.
Verywell Mind: According to an article on Verywell Mind, people who describe themselves as empaths also tend to care greatly about others. They understand the needs, wishes, and fears that others face, so an empath may strive to make sure that other people have the things they need to feel safe, secure, and happy.

2. Intuitive

Both narcissists and empaths have a heightened intuition. While empaths use this ability to understand and help others, narcissists use it to exploit and manipulate.

Medical News Today: According to an article on Medical News Today, empaths are people who feel more empathy than the average person. Empathy is the capacity to understand and share the feelings of others. Empaths also have the motivation to care for others’ well-being, coupled with their ability to recognize emotions.
Happier Human: According to an article on Happier Human, empaths often pick up on subtle, nonverbal cues about the feelings, thoughts, and emotions of people around them. They are very sensitive to the energy in a room and can quickly detect when a person is feeling uncomfortable or happy.
Coaching Online: According to an article on Coaching Online, what differentiates empaths from narcissists is that while empaths use this information to try to help other people, narcissists use this knowledge for their personal gains.

1. Difficulty with Boundaries

Narcissists often violate boundaries without remorse, whereas empaths struggle to establish boundaries because of their innate desire to help.

Narcissisms.com: According to an article on Narcissisms.com, narcissists feel entitled to violate boundaries, particularly with those they’re close to. At the beginning of a relationship or friendship, narcissists pretend to respect boundaries, but gradually they push more and more, getting you used to your boundaries being violated, further and further1.
Verywell Mind: According to an article on Verywell Mind, empaths should also work on knowing when and how to build up personal walls so that they aren’t always so easily absorbing the emotions of those around them. This won’t be easy, as creating boundaries likely doesn’t come naturally to empaths who are so driven to help.
Live Bold and Bloom: According to an article on Live Bold and Bloom, the empath will give beyond reasonable boundaries out of an innate desire to help; the narcissist needs unwavering support to fulfill their distorted sense of importance.

Like a perfectly choreographed dance between the sun and the moon, narcissists and empaths revolve around an intricate paradox. In unearthing these shared traits, we’ve not only made our journey through this unusual realm more illuminating but also highlighted the power of empathy and the complexity of narcissism. The uncanny reflection of narcissists in empaths and vice versa offers a valuable lesson — human personalities, however contrasting they may be, are intricately woven together in this rich tapestry of life. So, the next time you label someone a narcissist or an empath, remember: we’re all a paradox, a blend of darkness and light, shaped by the very traits that unite us.
