I keep reading about how people find AI emotionless, soulless, and so on.

Published on December 30, 2022

I keep reading about how people find AI emotionless, soulless, and so on. As someone who has spent years using AI to study emotion, I haven’t found this to be true of ChatGPT. It understands and can convey very complex emotion in its writing. One trick I use is developing character sheets. Not only do I use character sheets in fiction and ask my AI to make my characters consistent in the narrative and dialog, but I also use “Writing Voice and Style” character sheets for the AI to use in compositions like this one. The improvement is night and day. Also, just like we humans produce shitty first drafts in our writing, so does an AI. I’ve found that it’s style of “shitty” drafts compared to ours is generally better composed in many ways, but it is often devoid of emotion and soul unless you explicitly state that’s what you want on the first go. All AIs have limits on how many things they can focus on during a first draft and during revisions. How many things at once is a bit mysterious and takes some human intuition, but I have found that after 10 rewrites by an AI, with my guidance at every iteration, it can produce quite good, very acceptable, high quality, emotion filled, soulful work, that requires very little editing on my part. Just like the article you read. 🙂
