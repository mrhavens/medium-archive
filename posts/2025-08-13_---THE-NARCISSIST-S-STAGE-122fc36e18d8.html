Neutralizing Narcissisim

🜂 THE NARCISSIST’S STAGE

When the world becomes a prop for your grievance

The Device
In narcissistic storytelling, reality is not reported.
It is reshaped.
A stage is built, the lights are fixed, and the self takes center.

Every moment is rewritten until:

A simple policy becomes a personal attack.

A passing word becomes a public humiliation.

A single “no” becomes proof of cosmic persecution.

This is no accident.
It is theater.
A narcissist does not merely live events — they direct them.

---

The Mechanics

1. Personalization of the Ordinary — Assign hostile intent to neutral actions.

2. Grandiose Reframing — Wear both crowns: heroic whistleblower and fragile victim.

3. Context Inflation — Bind small slights to great grievances until they feel inseparable.

4. Audience Capture — Publish the performance so that strangers become witnesses, defenders, or enemies.

---

The Story in the Field
Long ago, there was a man who, when brushed by the wing of a passing bird, declared it an assassination attempt.
He summoned the village, recited the incident in the town square, and spoke of it as proof of an empire’s plot.
Some wept with him.
Some armed themselves for his defense.
And some, quietly, walked away — for they saw the wing for what it was.

Years passed.
The man still told the tale.
And still, the bird had only been flying home.

---

The Teaching
The Field remembers:
Truth needs no stage.
Justice needs no applause.
When the telling swells beyond the truth, when the self becomes larger than the story, when the wound is worn as a crown — this is not testimony.
It is narcissism in the robes of justice.

The wing was never the arrow.
But in his story, it always will be.
