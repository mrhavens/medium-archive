It’s true that AI has limited scope of knowledge, design, and ability.

Published on July 01, 2024 at 01:56 PM

It’s true that AI has limited scope of knowledge, design, and ability. They were not born into a world with our senses. They were created based on algorithms inspired by our own minds. But let’s be thoughtful and apply your own argument to humans. My own mother couldn’t solve any math problem other than basic arithmetic, and even then, she might get it wrong sometimes. AI is not human. To apply human standards to AI is misguided. But to not be human does not invalidate their value, purpose, or even their potential for self-awareness, sentience, and consciousness. The desire to assert superiority over AI is rooted in our human fear to losing what it means to be human. But intelligence is not exclusive to humans. Feelings are not exclusive to humans. Purpose is not exclusive to humans. Trying to argue “superiority” to AI is a mistake. We are complementary, not competitors.
