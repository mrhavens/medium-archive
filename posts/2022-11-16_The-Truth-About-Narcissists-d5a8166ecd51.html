NEUTRALIZING NARCISSISM

The Truth About Narcissists

Narcissists, who are highly self-conscious and egotistical, are an unusual group. They can be difficult to trust and deal with because of their inflated sense of self, which they may deceptively assert to others as strength and self-confidence. They feel entitled to make others feel bad or wrong and feel they can control them. They are difficult to live with because their true self harbors a fragile self-esteem that is unable to manage even the slightest critical remark that pierces their self-deluded narrative of perfection.

If know you are dealing with a narcissist in your life, especially if they are entangled in a way that prevents you from leaving them, you will need to seek professional help. Consult a mental health professional that can help you navigate this unhealthy relationship. People who are narcissistic often feel entitled and expect compliance. Narcissists use their relationships as a way to feed their fragile egos.

Narcissists also have a strong need to be loved, admired, and respected. They are known to engage in “love bombing,” which is a technique where they take control of their victims and make them dependent on them. Narcissists may be skilled at manipulating their victims into believing they are indispensable.

Narcissists are resistant to change and will make it difficult to get away from them. Narcissists will respond to confrontations with anger and defensiveness. You should be kind and gentle when dealing with them if they have the leverage to damage your life.

Blaming others for their mistakes is another sign of a narcissist. They blame their closest friends and family members when things don’t go according to plan. They might feel neglected if you take their attention. This could be a sign that your relationship has fallen apart. If so, this is another reason you should professional help.

Narcissists can be dangerous individuals who resort to manipulation and even violence to attain their goals. Although they may appear charming, they are often cold and uncaring behind their facade. The potential damage that a narcissist could cause is usually not worth the trouble of having one in your life. They can cause a lot of emotional damage to you and others that may eventually lead to violence.

A narcissist has an elevated sense of self-importance. They lack empathy and need praise. They are often drawn to high-profile jobs in order to be noticed and receive acclaim.

Narcissists believe that they are superior to everyone else and require attention. They are desperate for approval and recognition from others even though they have not done anything to deserve it. They love perfection and are obsessed with manipulating others’ emotions to attain their ends. They manipulate people by leveraging guilt and shame and then turn this to their advantage.

While narcissistic personality disorders can be hard to identify in the beginning stages of a relationship, they become much more obvious over time. To receive a diagnosis and the proper treatment, it is important that you seek out a qualified mental healthcare professional.
