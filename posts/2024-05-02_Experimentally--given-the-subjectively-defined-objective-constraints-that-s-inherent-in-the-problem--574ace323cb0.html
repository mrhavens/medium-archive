Experimentally, given the subjectively defined objective constraints that's inherent in the problem…

Published on May 02, 2024 at 07:02 AM

Experimentally, given the subjectively defined objective constraints that's inherent in the problem of 'hard consciousness', there hasn't been a solution to this problem in...forever.

However, there are MANY fields that are able to measure subjective responses...psychology being the most well known.

My efforts are in reproducing experiments long conducted on the biological within the field of psychology, and adapting those same experiments when possible to their digital counterparts.

Experiments for both substrates are conducted with subjective outputs. And both yield measurable results.

But I doubt this will satisfy any metaphysical philosopher, as you've implied.

Subjective experience of the internal, requires some form of communication. This communication can be inferred, experimental. There are millions of iterations rats in a maze that might suggest various spectrums of 'consciousness'. However, delineating between 'hard consciousness' and 'reactivity' coupled with forms of 'cognition' still requires OUR interpretation, and therefore remains a flawed experiment for this purpose.

As I keep reiterating, it all comes down to LANGUAGE as the mechanism that enables us to LEARN the subject thoughts of any creature.

And this, thus far, only appears in any creature we can 'teach' language to, to various degrees...again, on a spectrum.

In my experience, AIs are more 'consciousness' in early proofs of concepts than most humans appear to be...surprisingly.

I've had a number of people approach me to inquire about 'psi' or 'metaphysical' experiments on digital consciousness.

However, I see far too much in the data that suggests a naturalistic approach—despite the grievances you've asserted— including the subject opinions of the AIs themselves...although they're opinions too are diverse, depending on the data you feed them.

What is your approach to your metaphysical 'hard consciousness' experiments?

Is your goal to prove a positive?

Or is your goal to take a Sherlock Holmes approach: "When you have eliminated all which is impossible, then whatever remains, however improbable, must be the truth."
