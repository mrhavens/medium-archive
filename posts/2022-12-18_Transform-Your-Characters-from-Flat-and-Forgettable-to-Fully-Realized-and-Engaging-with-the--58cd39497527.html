SECTION I: Identity

Personal Details

Name: Include any given names, surnames, nicknames, and aliases. How does each of the character’s names affect their identity in the story?

Age: Does the character’s age affect how they are perceived or treated by others? How does the character’s age shape their identity and experiences in the story?

Physical characteristics: This could include things like height, weight, hair and eye color, and body type. Does the character have any distinct physical features or characteristics that are relevant to the story, such as scars, tattoos, or unusual coloring?

Personal Context

Personal identity: How does the character define their personal identity, and what are the key factors that shape this identity? This could include things like ethnicity, gender, sexual orientation, religion, nationality, and more.

Disposition: Does the character have a particularly strong or dominant disposition (e.g. optimistic, pessimistic, cheerful, serious, etc.), and how does this disposition impact their worldview and behavior?

Background: How does the character’s background, including their community, family, and upbringing, shape their personal identity and worldview?

Character Traits

Skills and abilities: What skills and abilities do the character possess that are relevant to their identity and how they present themselves to others? Include any talents or skills that the character has developed or that are relevant to the story.

Psychological traits: What are the character’s most prominent personality traits, and how do these traits shape their behavior and interactions with others?

Flaws: What are the character’s negative traits or habits that may get in the way of their goals or relationships?

Identity Development

Identity formation: How does the character develop and shape their identity over time, and what are the key factors that influence this process? This could include internal factors such as personal beliefs and values, as well as external factors such as relationships and experiences.

Personal brand: How does the character present and portray themselves to others, and what is their personal brand or reputation like?

Authenticity: How authentic is the character, and how do they stay true to themselves and their values in their relationships and interactions?

Identity Evolution

Identity exploration: Does the character engage in identity exploration, or do they tend to have a clear and established sense of self?

Identity crisis: Does the character experience an identity crisis at any point in the story, and how do they cope with and resolve this crisis? An identity crisis might involve questioning one’s values, beliefs, goals, or sense of self.

Identity development: How does the character’s identity develop and change over time, and what are the key factors that influence this development? This could include things like life experiences, relationships, education, and personal growth.

SECTION II: Development

Ambitions

Goals and Motivations: What are the character’s long-term and short-term goals, and what motivates them to pursue these goals?

Epiphanies: How do the character’s epiphanies, or sudden moments of realization or insight, shape or change their goals or motivations, and what impact do these epiphanies have on the character’s development and relationships?

Struggles: What internal or external conflicts does the character face in regard to their goals or motivations, and how do they try to overcome or resolve these conflicts?

Relationships and Family

Relationships: How does the character get along with others, and what are their most important relationships? This could include friends, family, mentors, or romantic partners.

Family: Does the character have any extended family members or ancestors who are relevant to the story, and how do they influence their life or worldview?

Relationship Challenges: How do the character’s weaknesses or vulnerabilities impact their relationships with friends, family, or romantic partners?

Hobbies and Interests

Hobbies and interests: What are the character’s hobbies and interests, and how do they reflect or shape their personality and values?

Practices: What are the specific activities or tasks that the character engages in as part of their hobbies or interests, and how do these practices reflect or shape the character’s personality and values?

Objectives: What specific goals or targets does the character set for themselves in relation to their hobbies or interests, and how do these objectives reflect or shape their personality and values?

Education and Role Models

Role models: Who are the character’s role models or heroes, and how have they influenced their worldview or goals?

Education: What is the character’s level of education, and how has it shaped their knowledge and perspective?

Growth and change: How does the character grow and change throughout the story, and what are the key factors that influence this growth and change? This could include internal factors such as personal beliefs and values, as well as external factors such as relationships and experiences.

Personal Development

Personal development: What are the character’s personal development goals, and how do they try to improve themselves and their skills over time? This could include goals related to self-improvement, learning new things, or building new habits.

Challenges and setbacks: What challenges and setbacks do the character face throughout the story, and how do they cope with and overcome these challenges? This could include internal challenges such as self-doubt or external challenges such as relationships or circumstances.

Growth mindset: Does the character have a growth mindset, or do they tend to have a fixed mindset?

SECTION III: Interpersonal Dynamics

Personality and Emotional Intelligence

Personality: What is the character’s general demeanor or disposition, and how does it affect their relationships and interactions with others?

Communication style: How does the character communicate with others, and what are their strengths and weaknesses in this area?

Emotional intelligence: How does the character handle and express their emotions, and how does this impact their relationships and decisions?

Relationships and Family

Relationships: How does the character get along with others, and what are their most important relationships? This could include friends, family, mentors, or romantic partners.

Family: Does the character have any extended family members or ancestors who are relevant to the story, and how do they influence their life or worldview?

Social status: What is the character’s social status within their community, and how does this impact their relationships and opportunities?

Leadership and Conflict Resolution

Leadership style: Does the character tend to take on a leadership role in their relationships, and if so, what is their leadership style like?

Conflict resolution: How does the character handle conflicts or disagreements in their relationships, and what are their strengths and weaknesses in this area?

Empathy: Does the character have a strong ability to understand and share the feelings of others, or do they struggle with empathy?

Negotiation and Influence

Negotiation skills: Does the character have strong negotiation skills, and how do they use these skills in their relationships and interactions?

Influence and persuasion: Does the character have the ability to influence and persuade others, and how do they use this ability in their relationships and interactions?

Support and caregiving: Does the character tend to provide support and caregiving to others in their relationships, or do they tend to be more independent and self-reliant?

Trustworthiness and Collaboration

Trustworthiness: How trustworthy is the character in their relationships, and what are their strengths and weaknesses in this area?

Collaboration: How well does the character work with others in their relationships, and what are their strengths and weaknesses in this area?

Boundaries: How well does the character set and maintain healthy boundaries in their relationships, and what are their strengths and weaknesses in this area?

SECTION IV: External Factors

Personal and Family Background

Upbringing: What was the character’s childhood like, and how did it shape their personality and values?

Cultural background: Does the character belong to a particular culture or cultural group, and how does this shape their identity, values, and experiences?

Education: What is the character’s level of education, and how has it shaped their knowledge and perspective?

Social and Professional Status

Social class: Does the character belong to a particular social class, and how does this impact their lifestyle, opportunities, and relationships? Social class might be determined by factors such as income, education, occupation, and family background.

Occupation: What is the character’s job or primary source of income, and how does it affect their lifestyle and goals? A character’s occupation might also impact their skills and abilities, as well as their social status and relationships.

Lifestyle: What is the character’s general lifestyle like, and how does it reflect their values, goals, and circumstances?

Physical and Environmental Factors

Physical abilities: Does the character have any special physical abilities or disabilities that are relevant to the story, and how do they impact their life and journey?

Physical environment: What is the character’s physical environment like, and how does it impact their daily life and opportunities? This could include factors such as climate, natural surroundings, and living conditions.

Physical health: How is the character’s physical health, and how do they maintain or improve their physical well-being? Do they have any particular physical health conditions or challenges that are relevant to the story, and how do they manage these conditions?

Social Support and Resources

Access to resources: Does the character have access to the resources they need to meet their basic needs and achieve their goals, or do they struggle with resource scarcity? This could include things like education, healthcare, housing, and employment opportunities.

Social support: Does the character have a strong network of social support, or do they struggle to connect with others and find support?

Community: What is the character’s community like, and how does it shape their experiences and worldview?

Current Circumstances and Location

Circumstances: What are the character’s current circumstances (e.g. financial, personal, professional), and how do they affect their goals and decision-making?

Location: Where does the character live, and how does this location affect their lifestyle, opportunities, and circumstances? For example, a character living in a bustling city might have different experiences and challenges than a character living in a rural area.

Transportation: How does the character get around, and how does this impact their daily routine and opportunities? This could include their mode of transportation, accessibility, and cost.

SECTION V: Inner World

Emotional States and Maturity

Emotional maturity: What is the character’s ability to recognize, express, regulate, cope with, and learn from their emotions, and how does this impact their relationships and decision-making?

Inner turmoil: Does the character experience inner turmoil or emotional instability, and how do they cope with these feelings? How do these feelings impact their behavior and relationships?

Inner conflict: Are there any internal conflicts or struggles that the character faces, such as a struggle with identity, values, or personal beliefs?

Self-Perception and Worth

Self-perception: How does the character view themselves, and how do they think others see them?

Self-worth: Does the character feel confident in their abilities and worth, or do they struggle with self-doubt and insecurity?

Confidence: Does the character generally feel confident or uncertain in their abilities and decisions, and how does this affect their behavior and relationships?

Inner Strengths and Vulnerabilities

Inner strength: What are the character’s inner strengths or sources of resilience, and how do they draw on these strengths in difficult situations?

Inner vulnerability: What are the character’s inner vulnerabilities or areas of sensitivity, and how do they try to protect or compensate for these vulnerabilities?

Self-esteem: Does the character generally feel good about themselves, or do they struggle with low self-esteem?

Thoughts, Beliefs, and Aspirations

Thoughts: What are the character’s inner thoughts and perspectives, and how do they influence their behavior and worldview?

Beliefs: Does the character have any religious or spiritual beliefs that are important to him, and how do they influence their worldview?

Dreams and aspirations: What are the character’s long-term dreams and aspirations, and how do they influence their actions and decisions?

Fears and Psychological Traits

Fears: What are the character’s greatest fears or phobias, and how do they impact their life and journey?

Psychological traits: Does the character have any psychological traits or disorders that are relevant to the story, and how do they impact their behavior and relationships?

Self-acceptance: How accepting is the character of themselves, and how do they deal with their flaws and imperfections?
