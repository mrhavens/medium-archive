Do you feel like your emotional needs are constantly being ignored or dismissed? You may be a victim of emotional withholding, a tactic used by narcissists to exert power and control over their victims.

Introduction

Emotional withholding is a tactic commonly used by narcissists to control and manipulate others. It involves deliberately withholding emotional support, affection, or validation as a way to punish or control the other person. Narcissists may use emotional withholding as a way to exert power and control over their victims, making them dependent on the narcissist for validation and emotional support. For survivors of narcissistic abuse, recognizing emotional withholding and learning how to address it is crucial for healing and moving forward. In this article, we will explore the impact of emotional withholding, the different ways it can manifest, and strategies for overcoming it.

Recognizing Emotional Withholding

Emotional withholding can manifest in many different ways, but at its core, it is a tactic used to control and manipulate others. Narcissists may use emotional withholding to make their victims feel dependent on them, to punish them when they don’t comply with their demands, or to exert power and control over them. It can be difficult to recognize emotional withholding, especially if you have been in a relationship with a narcissist for a long time. However, understanding the characteristics of emotional withholding and the ways it can manifest is crucial for healing and moving forward.

Feeling like you’re walking on eggshells

Imagine feeling like you’re walking on eggshells around someone, constantly afraid of saying or doing something that will cause them to withdraw their emotional support or affection from you. This is the feeling that many victims of emotional withholding experience. They are constantly second-guessing themselves, afraid of making a mistake that will cause the narcissist to withdraw their emotional support.

A game of power and control

Emotional withholding is like a game of power and control, where the narcissist holds all the cards and decides when and how to dispense emotional support, affection, or validation. This manipulation tactic is used to keep their victims in a state of constant uncertainty, always trying to please the narcissist in order to earn their emotional support.

A constant ebb and flow

Imagine a relationship where emotional support, affection, and validation are like a tide, constantly ebbing and flowing. One moment, the narcissist is showering you with attention and affection, and the next, they are cold and distant. This constant ebb and flow can be disorienting and exhausting for the victim, leaving them feeling unsure of where they stand in the relationship.

The impact of emotional withholding on mental health

Emotional withholding can have a significant impact on a person’s mental health. It can lead to feelings of self-doubt, anxiety, and depression. It’s important to recognize the impact of emotional withholding and take steps to address it in order to protect your mental health.

The power of setting boundaries

Setting boundaries is a powerful tool for overcoming emotional withholding. It allows you to take control of your emotional well-being and protect yourself from further abuse. It’s important to understand how to set boundaries and communicate them effectively.

The importance of seeking professional help

Seeking professional help is an important step in overcoming emotional withholding. A therapist or counselor can help you work through the emotional impact of the abuse and develop strategies for moving forward. They can also provide support and understanding in a non-judgmental setting.
