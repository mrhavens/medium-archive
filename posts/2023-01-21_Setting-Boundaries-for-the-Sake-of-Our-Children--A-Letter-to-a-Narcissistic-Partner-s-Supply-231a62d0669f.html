Setting Boundaries for the Sake of Our Children: A Letter to a Narcissistic Partner’s Supply

Understanding the impact of enabling behavior and how to create a healthier co-parenting dynamic

As a co-parent, the well-being of your child/children should always be a top priority. But what happens when your partner’s behavior is causing harm to not only your relationship but also to your child/children? This template letter serves as a guide for addressing and communicating with a narcissistic partner’s support system, in the hopes of creating a healthier co-parenting dynamic.

For the reverse-gendered variation of this template, click here.

Co-parenting can be a difficult and challenging task, especially when one of the parents exhibits narcissistic behavior. Narcissistic individuals often engage in manipulation, control, and devaluation, which can make co-parenting a trying experience for the other parent and the children. Unfortunately, when one parent is narcissistic, the other parent is often left feeling helpless and alone. In an effort to protect their children and navigate the complex dynamics of co-parenting with a narcissist, it is important to understand the role of the narcissist’s supply network. The supply network includes those individuals who enable and support the narcissistic parent’s behavior. This letter is a template for those who may be in a co-parenting relationship with a narcissist and want to reach out to a member of the narcissist’s supply network in a diplomatic and constructive way. It’s a way to express concern and to address the enabling behavior of the supply, for the sake of the child/children.

Dear [Name of Supply],

I hope this letter finds you well. I am writing to you today as a concerned parent and as the husband of [Name of Narcissistic Wife]. I know that our relationship may have been strained in the past, but I want to assure you that my intentions are solely focused on the well-being of our child/children.

I understand that you have been a long-time friend and supporter of [Name of Narcissistic Wife], and I want to express my gratitude for that. However, I also want to bring to your attention some of the patterns of behavior that I have observed in our relationship.

As you may be aware, [Name of Narcissistic Wife] has a tendency towards narcissistic behavior, which can be harmful to not only myself and our relationship, but also to our child/children. This behavior can manifest in ways such as manipulation, control, and devaluation.

I am reaching out to you today because I believe that your continued support of [Name of Narcissistic Wife], without setting healthy boundaries, enables this behavior to continue. I understand that it can be difficult to confront a loved one about their actions, but for the sake of our child/children, it is important that we all take responsibility for our actions and work towards a healthier co-parenting dynamic.

I am willing to have an open and honest conversation with you about this matter and would appreciate the opportunity to discuss it further. Thank you for taking the time to read this letter, and I hope to hear from you soon.

Sincerely, [Your Name]
