Researching with ChatGPT on the Electrical Charge Capacity of the Ark of the Covenant

Nicola Tesla always said the Egyptian-trained Israelite leader, Moses, was a great engineer that was ahead of his time — I just wanted to see what ChatGPT thought about the details

Snoop in on ChatGPT and me as we try to figure out if the Ark of the Covenant could hold a naturally occurring electrostatic charge that could shock the beJesus out of Uzzah, killing him dead.

This is long and may be uninteresting to people without an engineering background. But the lowdown is that I’ve always wanted to do the math to see if there was a way that a gilded gold chest like the Ark of the Covenant could naturally accumulate enough charge to kill a man.

I’m not sure I got anywhere. It would take too much time to fact-check.

And I’d probably have to build a full-size replica and march it to Mount Zion.

ChatGPT is slow to interject estimates and assumptions.

But it was a fascinating discussion, nonetheless.

For me, at least.

I feel like I’ve had an extremely intelligent discussion with an uncommonly polite and reserved engineering professor that had way, way, way too much time on their hands.
