The Dice-Based Whydoneit 5-Room Dungeon Generator: Create Your Own Crime-Solving Adventure

To use this generator, simply roll a die for each of the following categories: D4, D6, D8, D10, D12, and D100.

The number that you roll corresponds to the option listed for that die. For example, if you roll a 3 on the D4, you would use the third option listed under that category.

You can use this generator to create unique and random rooms for your D&D adventure. You have the option to use all or none of the 5 rooms in the 5 room dungeon, depending on your preference.

You can also customize and alter the options for each room to fit your specific needs.

Enjoy!

Room 1: The crime scene

The characters enter the dungeon and find a room containing the victim of a crime, along with clues as to the identity of the perpetrator.

D4: The type of crime that was committed:

1) murder, 2) theft, 3) assault, 4) vandalism

D6: The location of the crime scene:

1) a bedroom, 2) a kitchen, 3) a living room, 4) an office, 5) a workshop, 6) a basement

D8: The number of suspects:

1) one, 2) two, 3) three, 4) four, 5) five, 6) six, 7) seven, 8) eight or more

D10: The number of clues found at the crime scene:

1–3) a few clues, 4–6) several clues, 7–8) many clues, 9–10) an overwhelming amount of clues

D12: The state of the crime scene:

1–2) well-organized, 3–4) cluttered, 5–6) tidy, 7–8) messy, 9–10) chaotic, 11–12) pristine

D20: The presence of magical elements:

1–5) none, 6–10) a few, 11–15) several, 16–19) many, 20) an overwhelming amount

D100: The condition of the victim:

1–50) alive, 51–75) unconscious, 76–99) deceased, 100) missing
