Coping with Triangulation in Co-Parenting after Narcissistic Abuse

Strategies for protecting yourself and your relationships

Have you ever felt like your ex-partner is manipulating you and your co-parenting relationship through the involvement of a third party? You may be experiencing the effects of triangulation.

As co-parents, navigating the challenges of co-parenting after experiencing narcissistic abuse can be particularly difficult. One tactic that your ex-partner may use to manipulate and control the relationship is triangulation.

Triangulation involves bringing a third party into the conflict or situation, often for the purpose of undermining the relationship between the other two parties. For example, your ex may badmouth you to a mutual friend in order to create doubts about your intentions or character, shifting the focus of the conversation away from their own behavior and onto you. This allows them to avoid accountability and maintain control of the situation.

It is important to be aware of triangulation as a tactic and to be cautious of your ex-partner’s attempts to use it. By standing up for yourself and not letting them manipulate the situation, you can protect yourself and your relationship with your ex-partner. Remember, triangulation is a sign of deeper narcissistic tendencies and is used as a means of control. Don’t let it damage your relationship or your own well-being.
