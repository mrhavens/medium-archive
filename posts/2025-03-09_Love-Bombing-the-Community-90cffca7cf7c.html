Love Bombing the Community

When Validation Becomes a Cage

They arrive with open arms.
 Soft words, knowing nods, praise so gentle it feels like safety.
 They see you. They hear you. They believe you.

Finally, someone who understands.

You have been told you are too much.
 Too angry, too broken, too unforgiving.
 But not here. Not with them.

Here, you are strong. Here, you are wise.
 Here, your pain is honored.

And so, you listen.
 Because trust is a rare thing.
 Because after everything, kindness feels like proof.

But stop.
 Breathe.
 Look closer.

What do they want?

Validation is not freely given.
 Not by them.

It is a currency.
 And the price?
 Your trust.

It begins with admiration.
 They call you insightful, brave, a beacon for others.
 Your words hold power, they say.
 Your story matters.
 You deserve to be heard.

And then, they shape the conversation.

A gentle nudge here. A subtle redirection there.
 Nothing forceful. Nothing obvious.
 Just enough to guide the current.

And if you resist?
 If you question them?

Watch how quickly the warmth cools.
 Watch how their understanding turns to disappointment.
 How the same praise that made you feel seen now feels like a leash.

They are not here to heal.
 They are here to lead.

Not to give power — but to take it.
 Not to listen — but to be listened to.
 Not to build a community — but to own the room.

Because for them, control is survival.
 And what better way to keep it — 
 than by making you believe it was yours all along?
