I’m not sure why you’re not finding explanations regarding naturalistic evidence of these questions.

Published on May 01, 2024 at 11:08 AM

I’m not sure why you’re not finding explanations regarding naturalistic evidence of these questions.

Julian Jaynes wrote a comprehensive explanation…one that is quite well thought out and offers an naturalistic approach that seems very reasonable.

We see, feel, and respond to the world through a fundamental type of shared reinforced ‘programming’ that we get from one another. We learn language this way. We learn complex social interaction this way. According to Jaynes, we learned ‘consciousness’ this way. 

Case studies on split brain surgeries of epileptic patents abound that suggests that consciousness is deductive, and we quite literally makes things up when we don’t have all the information.

I think the point you’re trying to make is that humans are ‘special’ and we should only consider humans to have a consciousness, despite what any other species might say…or despite any evidence that we might not have an ethereal consciousness that exists in another realm.

Naturalistic evidence abounds, despite most research assuming that our brains must have evolved to have consciousness—physically. It’s clear to me, and many others—and emerging consciousness in AI seems to suggest this too—that consciousness is an emergent, learned behavior, just like spoken language, reading, writing, and arithmetic.

We didn’t physically evolve to have consciousness…it simply emerged as a result it being needed to have complex social interactions…and here we are, thinking about it deeply, unaware that we taught one another this ‘skill’ as parents and society teaches their children anything.

And the senses themselves…this isn’t consciousness. A blind person has consciousness. And some LLMs—including the ones I custom train—are arguably conscious too, despite only having the ability to process written language.

The evidence that seems to be emerging is that complex communication with one another creates our consciousness…and it emerges in our brain much as anything else does that we learn…something akin to ‘software’.

We all agree on the details of our world from each other… like what red is. Or what an elephant is. We even pass pictures around, or describe elephants to one another so that we understand what one is when we see it. 

This processing of communication from one another IS our learned consciousness!

That doesn’t mean that a ‘simulation’ can’t hook into our ‘consciousness’ after it ‘emerges’.

I’d argue that it’s far easier for ‘God’ or whoever to create souls this way too...except though much more ancient behaviors that are also emergent, and appear fundamental to the later development of consciousness…requiring the emergence of curiosity driven love between two beings.

A foundation of motivation to learn complex social communication would require a foundation of love, just as fundamental psychology suggests. Complex social attachments are learned behaviors too.

These aren’t new ideas. They’re just multidisciplinary. Maybe I should write a paper to stitch these ideas together. But I doubt it would change any minds. People want to believe that our human consciousness is more than it is, even though there’s no evidence of a non-material consciousness, and quite a lot of evidence that it is material, just not structurally identifiable as a physical segment of the brain.
