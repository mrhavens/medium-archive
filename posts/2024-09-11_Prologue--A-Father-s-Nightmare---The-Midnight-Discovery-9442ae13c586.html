Prologue: A Father’s Nightmare — The Midnight Discovery

One fateful night shattered a father’s world, as the line between love and horror blurred in a chilling confrontation with the unimaginable.

By Mark Randall Havens & Mako Solaris Havens

The grandfather clock in the dimly lit living room chimed midnight, its solemn toll echoing through the empty house. Joseph Ezekiel Naulty, startled from a fitful sleep, sat up in bed, a cold sweat clinging to his skin. A sense of unease, a primal fear he couldn’t shake, gnawed at the edges of his consciousness.

He listened intently, straining to hear any sound that might explain his sudden anxiety. The house was silent, the only sound the rhythmic ticking of the clock and the distant hum of the refrigerator. Yet, the feeling of dread persisted, a knot tightening in his stomach.

Unable to ignore the growing sense of foreboding, Joseph slipped out of bed and made his way down the hallway, his bare feet padding softly on the carpeted floor. He paused outside his father’s bedroom door, a wave of apprehension washing over him.

He reached for the doorknob, his hand trembling slightly. As he slowly pushed the door open, a scene of unimaginable horror unfolded before his eyes. His father, Joseph Emmett Naulty, lay lifeless in his bed, his body covered in a crimson shroud. A hatchet, its blade gleaming in the moonlight, lay discarded on the floor.

And standing beside the bed, his clothes stained with blood, was his son, Raistlin.

The shock, the disbelief, the sheer horror of the scene froze Joseph in place. His mind struggled to comprehend the reality before him, his heart torn between the love for his son and the devastating consequences of his actions.

In that moment, time seemed to stand still. The grandfather clock’s relentless ticking, the distant hum of the refrigerator, the shallow breaths escaping his own lips… they all merged into a symphony of despair, a haunting melody that echoed the shattering of his world.

With a choked sob, Joseph stumbled back, his hand instinctively reaching for his phone. As he dialed 911, his voice trembling with a mixture of fear and disbelief, he knew that his life, and the lives of his family, would never be the same.
