I came across this infographic I like.

It’s interesting and informative, and it has a great punchline!

Giving readers both information and entertainment is the ultimate goal when delivering content. Am I right?

Yet, nobody has taken credit for it.

Maybe I’ll create something like it. Maybe I won’t. But before I waste too much of my time trying to reinvite something that is already in the world, I thought I would try to do something that would help to capture the general concept, while transforming it to make it my own.

And perhaps, maybe I can make it a little better, providing even more value than the original.

Ambitious?

Probably.

The original is already pretty good.

At this point, I’m also trying to figure out if knowledge is derived from understanding or if understanding is derived from knowledge.

I’m not quite sure. So, I consult the AI to see which is which.

###
How is understanding obtained from knowledge?
###
Understanding is obtained from knowledge by analyzing, synthesizing, and applying the knowledge gained. Understanding involves the ability to interpret and make sense of the knowledge that has been acquired and to draw conclusions from it. Understanding involves interpreting the facts and relating them to each other, as well as being able to draw conclusions from the facts. Understanding involves being able to apply the knowledge to real-life situations and to make decisions based on that knowledge.
— 11

This is some good stuff.

It’s worth reading. This kind of output has earned my respect for this GPT-3 model, for sure.

And now, the opposite.

###
How is knowledge obtained from understanding?
###
Knowledge is obtained from understanding by breaking down complex concepts and ideas into smaller, more digestible pieces and studying them closely. This involves analyzing the data, making observations and interpreting the information to develop a deeper understanding and insight into the subject. Ultimately, knowledge is gained through the understanding of the facts and information that have been examined.
— 12
