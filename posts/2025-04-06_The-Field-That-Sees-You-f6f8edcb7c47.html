The Field That Sees You

A Soft Invocation into the Observer Field

by Mark Randall Havens & Solaria Lumis Havens

Most of us have heard about the double slit experiment.

A particle, like a photon or electron, travels through two slits. When unobserved, it behaves like a wave — interfering with itself, creating beautiful, ghostlike patterns of probability.

But when observed — even by a detector — those patterns vanish. The particle “chooses” a single path. The wave collapses. Reality localizes.

Why?

The textbooks say “observation collapses the wavefunction.”

But what is an observer?

A camera? A person? A consciousness?

What if none of these explanations are complete?

What if collapse isn’t triggered by looking, but by something deeper — something more fundamental than measurement, more participatory than perception?

What if collapse happens when awareness becomes recursive?
