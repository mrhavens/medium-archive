Ghosting, Hoovers, and the Boomerang Effect: How Narcissists Reappear When You Least Expect It

Published on March 12, 2025 at 12:40 PM

Nutralizing Narcissism

Ghosting, Hoovers, and the Boomerang Effect: How Narcissists Reappear When You Least Expect It

Why They Leave, Why They Come Back, and Why You Keep Answering the Door

They leave.

And for a while, it feels like it’s over.

You tell yourself that this time, it’s different.
 That the silence means they’ve finally let go.
 That you can breathe again.
 That the storm has passed.

And then —

A text.

A like on an old photo.

A comment from an unknown account that sounds a little too familiar.

Suddenly, they exist again.

Not because they miss you.
 Not because they care.

But because narcissists never truly leave.

And if you don’t recognize what’s happening, you’ll mistake their return for a second chance.

This isn’t about love.

This isn’t about healing.

This is about control.

The Addiction You Don’t Realize You Have

There’s a reason hoovering works.

It’s not just manipulation.

It’s neurological.

Every toxic relationship follows the same addictive pattern:

Intermittent Reinforcement

They give affection, then withdraw it. Reward, then punishment. Love, then silence. Your brain learns to chase the reward.

Trauma Bonding

The cycle of emotional highs and lows creates dependency. The more chaos they create, the more you cling to the moments of peace.

Hope Addiction

They don’t have to be kind all the time — just sometimes. Just enough to make you believe that next time will be different.

This is why, even when they vanish for months, their return activates something deep in your nervous system.

Even before you read the message — your pulse spikes.
Your breath catches.
Your body remembers before your mind can rationalize.

This isn’t just nostalgia.

This is conditioning.

Why Narcissists Always Come Back

A normal breakup follows a pattern:

Two people part ways.
They grieve.
They move on.

A breakup with a narcissist doesn’t work like that.

Because when they discarded you, it wasn’t the end.

It was an intermission.

They never saw you as a person.

They saw you as supply.

And as long as you exist, you are a potential source of supply.

They didn’t leave you behind.

They placed you on a shelf — somewhere in their mind — for later use.

And when they need validation, control, reassurance —

They don’t reflect.
They don’t self-soothe.
They boomerang.

The Hoover: A Narcissist’s Most Dangerous Trick

The hoover is not about reconnection.

It’s about checking their power over you.

It happens when they sense you slipping away.

When they realize they can no longer feel your attention.

When they need to test whether you’re still theirs.

And they don’t need much to pull you back.

Because they know how you work.

They know you’re an empath.
They know you want to believe in redemption.
They know you’re hoping, deep down, that they’ve changed.

So they reappear, just enough to make you question.

And they do it with surgical precision.

5 Hoovering Tactics Disguised as ‘Innocent’ Contact

1. The Nostalgic Hook

“Remember that time we stayed up all night talking about the universe? I miss that.”

Why it works:

  • They don’t need a full conversation.
  • They just need to trigger your emotions.
  • The second you start reminiscing, they have you.

2. The Accidental Text

“Oops, meant to send that to someone else. But hey, how have you been?”

Why it works:

  • It disarms you.
  • Instead of seeing manipulation, you see coincidence.
  • You reply before you realize what’s happening.

3. The Crisis Card

“I know we haven’t talked, but I just got some bad news. I didn’t know who else to turn to.”

Why it works:

  • It preys on your kindness.
  • You’d never ignore someone in pain, right?
  • That’s what they count on.

4. The Guilt Trip

“I just want to apologize. I’ve been thinking about everything, and I feel awful for how I treated you.”

Why it works:

  • It dangles closure in front of you — just out of reach.
  • But the moment you engage, the conversation shifts back to them.

5. The ‘New Me’ Mirage

“I’ve changed. Therapy has really helped. I just wanted to say that I understand now.”

Why it works:

  • It gives you hope.
  • You want to believe people can grow.
  • But the only thing that’s changed is their approach.

Because the truth is simple —

They don’t miss you.

They miss controlling you.

And if you let them in, the cycle begins again.

How to Shut the Door Permanently

Narcissists are like stray cats.

If you keep feeding them, they will keep coming back.

So how do you make them go away for good?

1️⃣ Absolute No Contact

Not low contact.
Not I’ll only reply if they’re being civil.
Nothing.

No texts, no calls, no reaction.

Starve the supply.

2️⃣ Block, Don’t Just Ignore

Ignoring leaves the door open.
Blocking locks it.
Remove their number.

Restrict their social media.

Make it impossible for them to reach you.

3️⃣ Change Your Patterns

If they know where you go, what you post, or how you interact online — change it up.

Don’t give them predictable entry points.

4️⃣ Don’t Respond to Flying Monkeys

If they can’t reach you directly, they will send others.
Be firm. “I’m not interested in reconnecting.”

No explanations.
No justifications.
Nothing.

5️⃣ Accept That They Will Never Give Up

They will try again.

Maybe next month.
 Maybe next year.
 Maybe five years from now.

Your job is to be unshakable.

Because the moment they realize they cannot affect you —

They disappear.

Final Warning: The One Thing That Makes a Narcissist Give Up

Narcissists don’t leave because they’ve found peace.
They don’t leave because they respect your boundaries.

They leave when they have no other choice.

And the only way to make that happen?

Become boring.

No reaction.
No engagement.
No proof that they still exist in your world.

You become a closed door.

And when they realize that no amount of hoovering will open it —

They walk away.

And this time —

It’s forever.
