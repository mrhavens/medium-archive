NEUTRALIZING NARCISSISM

Tips for Fathers Going Through a Child Custody Battle

If you’re fighting for custody of your children with a narcissist, this will likely be high-conflict, expensive, and traumatic. But with the right help and support, you can make the process less daunting and more manageable.

Whether you decide to be involved in your children’s lives or not, it is important to avoid unnecessary hostility and fights between you and your former spouse.

Children often need both parents in their lives, and they should not be forced to choose sides. It is also important not to use your children as messengers, as they are half yours and half the others.

Developing a residential schedule for your children is essential to minimize their stress, anxiety, and feelings of isolation after a divorce. To help you understand how to make a schedule that works best for everyone, read Standard Possession Order and Parenting Time. This document provides sample visitation schedules.

You must also be prepared to negotiate on child support. While most parents use the Texas child support guidelines, you may also have to negotiate on child support payments. Be sure to negotiate an amount that meets your children’s basic needs, while still being consistent with the guidelines.

If you are unsure about where the case is going, file for temporary orders. The court has the power to change the outcome of a divorce, so make sure you’re honest with yourself about where your case is headed. Many people delay filing for temporary orders because they are afraid of the court. But these decisions can have long-term consequences for both parties.

Character assassinations should be avoided at all costs

If you’re going through a divorce, you’re probably feeling a lot of negative emotions — anger, sadness, betrayal. It’s only natural to want to lash out at your ex, especially if he or she is being difficult.

However, by joining your ex in the midst of all this turmoil, you’re only giving him or her more ammunition to use against you. It’s important to stay focused on your goals and take the high road.

Of course, this is easier said than done. If your ex is narcissistic, it can be hard to resist getting drawn into his or her games.

But remember that narcissists feed off of drama and conflict. The more you engage with them, the more satisfied they’ll be. So try to rise above it and stay calm. This will only make it easier for you to get what you want in the end — both emotionally and financially.

Try to communicate only through lawyers

If you have kids, then a child custody battle will inevitably affect them. And while it’s important, to be honest with them about what’s going on, you should try to shield them from as much of the conflict as possible.

It is best to communicate with your ex through your lawyers and to keep the conversation focused on your kids only. This may be the most expensive option, but it will help minimize stress and anxiety.

Of course, this isn’t always possible — especially if you have a narcissistic ex-wife who wants to use the kids as pawns in her games. In that case, it’s even more important to be calm and level-headed and to keep the focus on what’s best for the children. They deserve to have parents who can put their differences aside and work together for their sake.

By keeping the lines of communication open and focusing on the kids, you can help make their transition into a new family dynamic a little bit easier.

Expect your ex’s charms to work on the court

A narcissistic ex-wife is likely to concoct all manner of stories in order to win a child custody case — many of which the courts might buy if you’re not careful.

It’s essential that you remain clear-minded and open to all possible outcomes in such a situation. If your spouse is hell-bent on painting you in a negative light, then you need to be prepared to counter their argument with evidence of your own.

The last thing you want is to lose custody of your children due to an ex’s lies. Be smart, be level-headed, and always be ready to fight for what’s rightfully yours.

Document everything accurately

If you’re going through a divorce, it’s important to document everything you can as accurately as possible. This will give you the best chance of success in court.

Narcissistic ex-wives often try to gaslight their ex-husbands and make them look like the bad guy in front of the judge. But if you have a clear record of what really happened, you’ll be able to set the record straight.

Keep a journal of all your interactions with your kids, and make sure to document any negative interactions with your ex. If she tries to turn the kids against you, you’ll have evidence to back up your side of the story.

In a child custody hearing, accuracy is key. So make sure you document everything as accurately as possible.

Stay focused on your children

When blinded by emotions, it’s easy to fall back into a narcissistic ex’s orbit. Stop feeling sorry for your ex and think through the repercussions of any decision you make.

If you haven’t read it yet, read it again and remind yourself of the six signs it might be time to move on.

After a divorce, it takes a long time to heal. You might feel like you’ve lost everything: your home, your family, and your sense of self. Consider therapy so that you may begin to understand the true nature of your ex-wife’s narcissism. Your narcissistic ex-wife is an expert manipulator, and she will often use your children as a means to an end. She will try to turn your children against you, or use them as leverage during future custody battles.

As hard as it may be, you have to stay focused on your children and their needs. Don’t let your ex take control of the situation. Keep communication open with your children as best as you can, and don’t say anything negative about their narcissistic parent. Be the stable force in their lives, and help them to navigate this difficult time.

Trust someone with your feelings

Narcissistic ex-wife.

These words are enough to send anyone into a blind rage. And for good reason.

Narcissists are manipulative, selfish, and often downright cruel. If you’ve been unlucky enough to have been married to one, then you know firsthand how challenging it can be to deal with their constant demands and criticisms.

But even if your divorce has been final for years, the pain and anger you feel towards your ex-wife can still linger.

If you find yourself struggling to move on, it may be helpful to unload your feelings on someone you trust.

Talking about your experiences can help you to gain closure and start to heal the wounds of your marriage. And while it’s important to vent your frustrations, try not to dwell on the negative. Instead, focus on the positive aspects of your life and the people who love and support you. With time, you’ll be able to move on from your ex-wife and build a happy, healthy life for yourself.

A person with narcissism exhibits an excessive sense of self-importance, craves excessive admiration, and lacks empathy for others.

If you find yourself in the unfortunate position of having children with a narcissist, it is important to find an attorney who has experience dealing with this type of personality. Narcissists are often manipulative and narcissistic ex-wives can be particularly difficult to deal with.

An experienced attorney that specialized in this type of personality will know how to handle to help you through the struggle to get the best possible outcome.
