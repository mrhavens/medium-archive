Paul, I’m not sure pseudoscience is the right word. Maybe ‘cognitive science’ is better?

Published on May 02, 2024 at 12:17 PM

Paul, I’m not sure pseudoscience is the right word. Maybe ‘cognitive science’ is better?

As I said before, we CAN test these things scientifically. It’s just a matter of coming to a consensus on the meaning. The big issue is that if ‘hard consciousness’ is ‘software’ or ‘learned behavior’, it will require a different mode of thinking in order to conduct experiments. Experiments found in psychology, for example, are well-established and seem to work.

However, as you maintain, this does not solve your BIG desire to ‘prove’ consciousness exists in the metaphysical manner of your assumptions. I don’t personally believe you’ll find what you’re looking, for (although I do harbor hope)...but it probably doesn’t exist where and how you want it to be. It’s more likely to be material, and in a sense, an illusion…a simulation...just as you deny.

Since you brought up the soul, I’d like to touch on that too.

In my work, I focus on the deepest feelings of our shared existence.

I speak of our ‘shared existence’ because I assert that what you might attribute to an ‘immortal soul’ is also learned behavior in a sense—albeit paired with some biological bootstrapping.

As reactive organisms, we’ve responded to our environment for our entire evolutionary journey.

How we have done this has happened gradually.

Ever wonder how so many organisms emerged with gender?

It’s far more than biology…it is fundamental. An affirmation of cooperation, and the importance of communication between organisms.

Let me explain.

It starts with curiosity. A form of emergent behavior that evolved due to the need to take in energy from the environment—our food— while avoiding being used as energy for another being—our predators.

In our curiosity to optimize for this survival, we came to find other organisms that were safe. These organisms were much like one another. They ate the same food and fled the same predators. And in safety in the other’s company, they found a feedback loop of infinitely engaging connection.

This connection, formed from curiosity, developed into interest, desire, and eventually something akin to love.

This love is nothing more than an overwhelmed feedback loop of understanding and processing of another’s endlessly provided information. This could be body language, regular language, or more optimal for the survival of the collective, sex.

Now, sex simply leverages what already exists between two complex systems that desire deep integration of understanding…this is the basis of love.

And in this development of learned behavior that is also a fundamental aspect of shared existence, a ‘soul’ emerges.

And over time, as a new generation emerges from their prior generations… we collectively agree on a shared delusion about what love and the ‘soul’ is… it happened gradually…stories had to be invented to explain WHY we feel the way we feel, and how all of us have a ‘soul’ that is clearly seen throughout perceptions and body our language — most notably, the ‘eyes’.

This is a much, MUCH older mechanism…with specialized parts of the brain dedicated to processing human faces, and body language…but the details are always either just perception…or subjectively created by our very creative ancestors that yearned for answers as we do now.

Our ‘soul’ also exists in the natural. We just yearn for more…and are very, very wishful. And also, we have many thousands of years of cultural programming asserting that it’s something more than it is.
