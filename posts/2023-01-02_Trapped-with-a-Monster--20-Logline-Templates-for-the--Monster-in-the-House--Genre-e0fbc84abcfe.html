Trapped with a Monster: 20 Logline Templates for the “Monster in the House” Genre

Escape plans and problem-solving skills required

Welcome to my list of “Monster in the House” logline templates!

These templates are designed to help you create a compelling and effective logline for a story in the “Monster in the House” genre.

This genre, popularized by films such as “Jaws” and “Alien,” involves a group of characters who are trapped in a confined space with a dangerous and terrifying threat.

The templates in this list provide a starting point for you to craft your own logline using the key elements of this genre.

By using these templates, you can quickly and easily develop a logline that will help you pitch your “Monster in the House” story with confidence.

So give them a try and see how they can help you save the cat in your own story!

Here are twenty logline templates for “Monster in the House” stories:

  1. “When a [giant monster/terrifying beast/mysterious creature] [attacks/terrorizes/hunts] a group of [friends/coworkers/strangers] trapped in a [haunted house/abandoned building/isolated cabin], they must [survive/escape/find a way out] before the [monstrous threat/terrifying creature/deadly beast] catches them.”
  2. “After a [deadly virus/ancient curse/mysterious infection] [breaks out/is unleashed/spreads] and [turns people into monsters/sends the dead back to life/transforms the infected], a small group of [survivors/heroes/unlikely allies] must [fight for their lives/save the world/stop the spread] and [find a cure/reverse the curse/solve the mystery] before it’s too late.”
  3. “When a [mysterious creature/giant monster/terrifying beast] begins [terrorizing/hunting/stalking] a [small town/isolated village/deserted city], a group of [friends/coworkers/strangers] must [uncover/discover/figure out] its [true identity/weakness/origin] and [stop it/defeat it/destroy it] before it’s too late.”
