Entitlement Unchecked: The Expectation of Special Treatment

Navigating the One-Way Street of Narcissistic Entitlement

Journey through the understanding of the narcissistic sense of entitlement, a trait that demands superior treatment and questions the emotional traffic of give-and-take in relationships.

Entitlement in the realm of narcissistic behavior is often not a loud proclamation but a quiet, pervasive expectation. It is the silent assumption that they should be first in line, the unspoken demand for deference, and the invisible force that bends the will of others to prioritize their needs above all else.

This sense of entitlement can be a glaring red flag, indicating the presence of narcissism within a loved one. It is a trait that does not merely seek but assumes special treatment as a due right, not a privilege earned. The question then arises, “Is this expectation of special treatment a symptom of my loved one’s narcissistic personality?”

In relationships with a narcissist, the expectation of entitlement creates a one-way street where all the signs point in one direction — towards their benefit. It’s a path where reciprocity is missing, and the usual traffic of give-and-take is rerouted to accommodate only the narcissist’s desires.

Unchecked entitlement takes its toll on relationships. It creates an imbalance where one party constantly receives, while the other is left empty-handed. Partners and loved ones often feel like mere satellites orbiting the narcissist’s world, their own needs eclipsed by the constant shadow of the narcissist’s demands.

To recognize whether this unchecked entitlement is part of a loved one’s narcissistic tendencies, it is essential to observe the dynamics of interactions. Does the person consistently place their needs above others? Do they react with indignation when not treated as they expect? These are the signs that indicate the trait of entitlement.

The emotional impact on those who cater to the narcissist’s sense of entitlement can be profound. It often leads to feelings of resentment, diminished self-worth, and an erosion of the mutual respect that is foundational to any healthy relationship.
