The Scorpion and the Toad-tally Toxic Friendship

FABLE-OUS HAVENS TALES FOR THE MODERN AGE

The Scorpion and the Toad-tally Toxic Friendship

A warning about trust and self-care

When a skeptical frog gets stung after reluctantly accepting help crossing a river from a manipulative scorpion, the frog learns to be more cautious in trusting others while the scorpion vows to be more honest about its harmful nature.
For my little rabbit,
May this fable remind you to always be mindful of who you trust and to take care of yourself. May it also inspire you to explore the world around you, to ask questions, and to learn new things.
With love and a lifetime of adventures ahead,
Mark Randall Havens
Tuesday, November 29, 2022

The scorpion and the frog were having a discussion about crossing a river. Having helped a turtle cross a river before, the scorpion said that scorpions were perfectly suited to advise frogs on swimming across rivers and that a frog should never navigate a river alone.

But the frog was not convinced. The frog was skeptical of the scorpion because scorpions had a reputation for stinging other animals. The frog said that he had been swimming in ponds ever since he was a baby tadpole and that rivers couldn’t be much different.

“But ponds and rivers are not the same,” the scorpion warned the frog. “A river has a perilous current, while a pond does not.”

As the frog listened to the scorpion’s words, he tilted his head to the side and furrowed its brow in thought. It was true that the frog had been swimming in ponds for many, many years, but the scorpion’s warning about the perilous current of the river caused him to hesitate. The frog could see the concern in the scorpion’s eyes, and he knew that it was not an animal to be taken lightly. Still, the frog’s mistrust persisted despite the scorpion’s assurances. After all, scorpions had a reputation for stinging other animals, and the frog wasn’t sure he was willing to take the risk.

“A river would be much more difficult to swim than a pond,” the scorpion continued. “And only with my guidance and your cooperation will you be able to safely transverse this river.”

The frog asked, “How can I trust that your guidance will be of value since you yourself don’t even know how to swim?”

There was a momentary flash of anger in the scorpion's demeanor, but it quickly shifted and became much more pleasant.

“I have already helped a turtle in desperate need of my wisdom navigate not just one river, but two rivers, just like this one. And because of my guidance, we together made it to the other side on both occasions,” the scorpion asserted. “I can most certainly help you cross this river just like I helped the turtle.”

“But you can not harm a turtle because of its shell,” the frog said. “If I carry you on my back, and you sting me, I will surely die.”

The scorpion leaned in closer, speaking quietly, “If I sting you while we are crossing the river, we will both surely die.”

The frog paused to think, considering the scorpion’s words, and then agreed.

As the scorpion and the frog began to cross the river, the scorpion delivered a venomous sting right where it stood on the frog’s back.

As the scorpion and the frog began to sink, the frog yelled, “Why did you sting me? Now we are both going to die!”

“I’m so sorry, frog. I shouldn’t have done this,” the scorpion replied, “I just couldn’t resist the temptation to sting. It’s my nature, and I couldn’t go against it.”

“But why did you agree to help me cross the river in the first place?” The frog asked while flailing in the water.

“I wanted to prove that I could be helpful and trustworthy, even though I’m a scorpion,” the scorpion replied. “But I acknowledge now that I should have been more upfront about my limitations and made it clear that you were taking a risk by accepting my help.”

As the scorpion and the frog continued to sink, the frog struggled to keep his head above water. He flapped and kicked his webbed feet with all his might, trying to swim to shore. But the frog had become too weak from the effects of the scorpion’s venom.

Just when it seemed like all hope was lost, a group of nearby animals heard the commotion and rushed to help. A nearby beaver, quickly noticing the situation, used its strong teeth to cut a sturdy branch from a nearby tree. A squirrel, seeing the beaver’s efforts, quickly ran over and used its nimble paws to tie the branch to the frog’s back.

The beaver and squirrel worked together, with the beaver pulling on the branch and the squirrel, holding it steady. Despite the powerful flow of the river, they were able to inch closer and closer to the shore.

As they reached the riverbank, the beaver used its powerful front legs to heave the branch onto dry land, pulling the frog and scorpion to safety.

After the beaver and squirrel rescued the frog and the scorpion from the river, the frog turned to the scorpion and said, “I forgive you for stinging me, but I also have to protect myself from being hurt again in the future. I hope that you can understand and respect the importance of trust and cooperation in our relationships with each other.”

The scorpion nodded, realizing the error of its ways. “You are right, frog. I should have been more upfront about my limitations and made it clear that you were taking a risk by accepting my help. I promise to be more honest and considerate in the future.”

As the group walked back to their homes, the beaver and squirrel were hailed as heroes for their bravery and selflessness in rescuing the frog and the scorpion. All the animals praised their quick thinking and teamwork.

From then on, the frog approached new situations with caution, always taking the time to assess the risks and to consider the intentions of those around him. But at the same time, he also learned to trust and rely on others, forming strong bonds and overcoming challenges together. The frog and scorpion remained friends, working together to navigate the twists and turns of life, always remembering the importance of trust and caution.

The frog and the scorpion learned an important lesson about trust and cooperation, and they vowed to always consider the needs and feelings of others in their relationships. And the beaver and squirrel were forever remembered as heroes for their selflessness and bravery.

In this fable, we learn that it is important to be careful about who we trust and to set boundaries with those who may not have our best interests at heart. We also see that trust must be earned, and honesty about our limitations and intentions is crucial in building trust with others. Remember these lessons as you navigate your own relationships and strive to build trust with those around you.
