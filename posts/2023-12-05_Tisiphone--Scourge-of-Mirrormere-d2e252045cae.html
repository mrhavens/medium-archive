Tisiphone: Scourge of Mirrormere

Chronicles of the Guardian Avenger

When a young noble commits murder to claim a throne, he finds himself haunted by Tisiphone, an unrelenting and terrifying avatar of vengeance who drives him to madness to repay his betrayal.
“I am the balance keeper. My whip seeks those who disrupt the scales.”

In the shadowed realms of Mirrormere, where the lakes mirror the sky and the forests whisper ancient secrets, Tisiphone, the Guardian of Retribution, prowled. Her wings, grand and draconic, cast eerie shadows upon the land as she soared, her eyes a fiery abyss, searching.

In the village of Eldenmere, a crime had shaken the very foundations of justice — a murder most vile, a betrayal of blood. A young noble, Alaric, had succumbed to greed, poisoning his kin for a throne not his to claim. Tisiphone had heard the silent cries of the departed, and she answered.

Alaric, cloaked in newfound power and blind arrogance, celebrated his ill-gotten gain. But as night fell, so did a suffocating dread. The air grew cold, the candles flickered, and then she appeared — Tisiphone, a spectral vision of vengeance.

Her presence filled the room, a pressure that clawed at Alaric’s sanity. Her eyes, dark as the abyss, bore into him, unearthing his deepest fears. The serpents in her hair hissed, their tongues tasting the air, savoring the scent of guilt.

“Alaric of Eldenmere,” her voice was a whisper, yet it thundered in his ears, “You have stained your soul with kin’s blood. The scales of justice tip against you.”

Alaric, once proud and haughty, cowered. He pleaded, he wept, but Tisiphone’s gaze never wavered. Her whip, entwined with thorns and serpents, materialized in her hand, its presence promising retribution.

With a crack that shattered the silence, the whip lashed. Not upon his flesh, but his mind. Visions of his crime, the dying gasp of his kin, the weight of his betrayal — all flooded into him. Tisiphone’s power drove him to the brink of madness, each lash a reminder of his guilt.

The night waned, and with it, Alaric’s sanity. He was found at dawn, a shell of a man, whispering confessions to the wind. Tisiphone, her justice delivered, vanished into the mists of Mirrormere, her wings fading into the morning light.

In Eldenmere, they spoke of that night in hushed tones, a tale of horror and awe. Tisiphone, the Guardian of Retribution, had exacted justice, her power both terrifying and sublime. In Mirrormere, the balance had been restored, but the fear of her wrath lingered, a thrilling specter in the hearts of all.
